# T2108

A market-breadth indicator — the percentage of NYSE stocks trading above their 40-day simple moving average — that Dr. Wish uses for overbought / oversold context alongside the [GMI](gmi.md).

## What it is

**Definition:** T2108 measures "the percentage of all NYSE stocks that have closed above their simple moving average of closes the past 40 days." ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

**Source:** Available through the Worden / TC2000 charting platform (enter symbol `T2108`), and also via the free site freestockcharts.com. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

Dr. Wish describes it as a pendulum: "Markets tend to be extended up when the T2108 is above 80%. When it is below 10%, the market is usually close to the bottom of a steep decline." ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

## How he uses it

### Thresholds

| Level | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| > 80% | Extended / overbought; approach with caution |
| ~70% | "High neutral territory" |
| ~50% | Neutral |
| < 10% | Near bottom of a steep decline; contrarian buy zone |

At T2108 < 10%, his stance is to "grit my teeth while the market gossip is terrible and buy a market index ETF." He notes the market (though not all individual stocks) has always recovered from such extremes. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

### Asymmetry: high readings are less actionable than low readings

A high T2108 reading is "not as predictive as an extremely low reading, below 10%." An extended market can stay extended for a long time; a deeply oversold market tends to bounce quickly. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))

In September 2009, T2108 reached 89% — staying elevated without triggering a crash. In August 2010, it was ~80% again with the same assessment: near overbought but not a strong sell signal. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))

By March 2011, T2108 was at 70% — described as "high neutral territory," with 80% being where short-term tops tend to occur. ([WW 2011-03-07](../../raw/posts/2011-03-07-introducing-the-gmi2-tc2000-com-ibd50-stock-performance-put-options-on-lulu.md))

### Combination with the GMI

T2108 is published alongside the GMI in Dr. Wish's weekly table. It supplements the GMI as a breadth check: a high GMI with a very low T2108 would be an unusual combination worth watching; a very low T2108 with a declining GMI is the setup for a potential bounce buy in index ETFs. See [market-state playbook](../playbooks/market-state.md).

## Evolution

The T2108 indicator has appeared in Dr. Wish's posts from at least 2010 onward without major changes to the thresholds. The 80% / 10% reference levels appear consistently. The 2014-08-03 post gives the most complete definition.
