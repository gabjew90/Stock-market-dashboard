# T2108

A market-breadth indicator — the percentage of NYSE stocks trading above their 40-day simple moving average — that Dr. Wish uses for overbought / oversold context alongside the [GMI](gmi.md).

## What it is

**Definition:** T2108 measures "the percentage of all NYSE stocks that have closed above their simple moving average of closes the past 40 days." ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

**Source:** Available through the Worden / TC2000 charting platform (enter symbol `T2108`), and also via the free site freestockcharts.com. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

Dr. Wish describes it as a pendulum: "Markets tend to be extended up when the T2108 is above 80%. When it is below 10%, the market is usually close to the bottom of a steep decline." ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

## How he uses it

### Thresholds

| Level | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| > 80% | Extended / overbought; approach with caution |
| ~70% | "High neutral territory" |
| ~50% | Neutral |
| < 10% | Near bottom of a steep decline; contrarian buy zone |

At T2108 < 10%, his stance is to "grit my teeth while the market gossip is terrible and buy a market index ETF." He notes the market (though not all individual stocks) has always recovered from such extremes. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))

**Precise below-10% action (2022):** when T2108 drops below 10%, the specific accumulation method is: buy **SPY** (the broad market index ETF, not individual stocks), starting small, and accumulating **only on the way up** after a bottom appears to form. He does not try to catch the exact bottom. ([WW 2022-05-01](../../raw/posts/2022-05-01-blog-post-based-on-my-analysis-of-the-market-it-is-nowhere-oversold-enough-to-be-near-a-bottom-here-is-the-ev.md))

**Monthly chart context (2022):** Dr. Wish monitors T2108 on a **monthly** chart to assess the longer-term breadth regime. In May 2022, T2108 was at 27% on the monthly chart — "nowhere near" the below-10% level that would signal a bottom, supporting his view that the 2022 decline had much further to go. The monthly view filters out short-term noise and tracks the bear-market breadth trajectory. ([WW 2022-05-01](../../raw/posts/2022-05-01-blog-post-based-on-my-analysis-of-the-market-it-is-nowhere-oversold-enough-to-be-near-a-bottom-here-is-the-ev.md))

**2018 contrast:** the February 2018 correction took T2108 to 8.6% (below 10% = extreme oversold signal), while the March 2018 GMI-Red signal found T2108 at only 26% (still not at the panic level). This demonstrates how the same 30-day index decline can produce very different T2108 readings. ([WW 2018-03-25](../../raw/posts/2018-03-25-time-for-cash-gmi1-of-6-turns-red.md))

### Asymmetry: high readings are less actionable than low readings

A high T2108 reading is "not as predictive as an extremely low reading, below 10%." An extended market can stay extended for a long time; a deeply oversold market tends to bounce quickly. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))

In September 2009, T2108 reached 89% — staying elevated without triggering a crash. In August 2010, it was ~80% again with the same assessment: near overbought but not a strong sell signal. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))

By March 2011, T2108 was at 70% — described as "high neutral territory," with 80% being where short-term tops tend to occur. ([WW 2011-03-07](../../raw/posts/2011-03-07-introducing-the-gmi2-tc2000-com-ibd50-stock-performance-put-options-on-lulu.md))

### Combination with the GMI

T2108 is published alongside the GMI in Dr. Wish's weekly table. It supplements the GMI as a breadth check: a high GMI with a very low T2108 would be an unusual combination worth watching; a very low T2108 with a declining GMI is the setup for a potential bounce buy in index ETFs. See [market-state playbook](../playbooks/market-state.md).

## Evolution

The T2108 indicator has appeared in Dr. Wish's posts from at least 2008 onward without major changes to the thresholds. The 80% / 10% reference levels appear consistently. The 2014-08-03 post gives the most complete definition. The 2008-10-06 post provides the earliest systematic historical comparison of T2108 extreme lows.

## Code — computing T2108

T2108 is one line of arithmetic over a universe of price series ([`src/ww/indicators/t2108.py`](../../src/ww/indicators/t2108.py)): the percentage trading above their 40-day simple moving average. The catch is the *universe* — T2108 proper is computed across (essentially) all NYSE-listed stocks, which is bulk data the free provider can't supply, so `t2108(provider, date, universe=...)` raises `DataUnavailable` with `YFinanceProvider`. But the formula itself runs on any basket:

```python
def t2108_from_prices(price_frames, date, *, window=40) -> float:
    above = total = 0
    for df in price_frames.values():
        s = df["close"].astype(float).loc[:pd.Timestamp(date)]
        if len(s) < window:
            continue
        total += 1
        if s.iloc[-1] > s.rolling(window).mean().iloc[-1]:
            above += 1
    return 100.0 * above / total
```

So you can compute a *miniature* T2108 over, say, a handful of large caps as a demonstration — it's the same calculation, just on a tiny universe. `ww compute t2108 2014-08-01 --demo` does this against built-in illustrative fixtures. Computing the real, full-NYSE T2108 (and thus reproducing his historical readings) needs a bulk-equity data feed — a later phase.

## Reconstructing T2108 from free data

`data/breadth/breadth_series.parquet` (built by `ww breadth build`) carries two T2108-equivalents — `t2108_nyse` (% of *NYSE* common stocks above their 40-day SMA, closest to Worden's actual T2108 definition) and `t2108_broad` (the same over the broader NYSE+Nasdaq+AMEX common-stock universe, closest to his GMI's ~4,000-stock universe), each computed point-in-time per date from a panel of ~4–5k stocks pulled via yfinance. `ww breadth validate` compares both against the T2108 readings Dr. Wish reported in his daily posts: as of the last run, the **nyse** flavor fit best — Pearson correlation ≈ 0.932, RMSE ≈ 10.7 percentage points, mean bias ≈ +4.4 (ours − his). The broad-universe flavor has marginally lower RMSE (9.85 pp, corr 0.930) and lower bias (+2.7 pp). Both flavors read optimistically because the universe is the *current* listing — stocks that delisted before today are absent, which inflates past breadth readings.

Despite the level bias, the reconstruction correctly captures crisis-regime extremes: on 2008-10-10 it computes T2108-equiv ≈ 1.7% (NYSE) / 2.9% (broad), and on 2020-03-23 ≈ 1.1% / 2.8% — consistent with his documented single-digit readings for both crashes. The practical implication: use the reconstruction for regime-state identification (above/below 20%, approaching 80%) rather than precise level comparisons with his published numbers.

Run it: `ww compute t2108 2026-05-11 --breadth`, or `ww breadth show --date 2008-10-10`.
