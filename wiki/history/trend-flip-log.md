# Trend-flip log

A systematic record of every documented QQQ short-term trend flip (Day-1 posts) found in the blog corpus, plus long-term stage transitions (30-week average), with Dr. Wish's reaction to each. Read in conjunction with [Timeline](timeline.md) and [Track record](track-record.md).

## 1. How he reacts to trend flips — and whipsaws

### Short-term flips: tactical, confirmation-gated, trading account only

A QQQ short-term flip — from up-trend to down-trend or vice versa — triggers a tactical response in the **trading IRA only**. The university pension is governed by a separate, slower protocol (see below).

On **Day 1 of a new down-trend**, Dr. Wish typically takes a small position in the 3× inverse ETF SQQQ (or TWM for IWM exposure) and raises stops on any remaining longs. He does not immediately go to full cash: "About one quarter of new short term down-trends have lasted less than 6 days." He adds to the inverse position only after **Day 5**, once the probability of a quick reversal has dropped substantially. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md), [WW 2019-04-07](../../raw/posts/2019-04-07-trading-ipos-with-a-glb-and-a-green-dot-signal-iipr-qqq-short-term-up-trend-is-in-61st-day.md))

On **Day 1 of a new up-trend**, he buys a small position in TQQQ and adds as the trend confirms. "I especially like to buy the bullish leveraged ETF on the **first day** of a new QQQ short-term up-trend. I wade in slowly and buy more as the index confirms the trend. Most confident at Day 5." ([WW 2011-01-04](../../raw/posts/2011-01-04-2010-etf-performance-why-search-for-individual-stocks-when-one-can-just-ride-the-leveraged-etfs.md)) The [GMI](../methodology/gmi.md) buy signal requires two consecutive days above 3; he waits for that confirmation before committing heavily. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))

The [QQQ Short-Term Timing](../methodology/qqq-short-term-timing.md) signal is triggered by the QQQ closing above or below its **30-day moving average**. Because closing prices are used — not intraday prices — he can only act after the close, preventing whipsaw on intraday spikes. He never uses hard stops for GLB positions during a down-trend; mental stops and the stage framework govern those decisions. ([WW 2021-01-24](../../raw/posts/2021-01-24-blog-post-in-the-60s-i-used-to-receive-a-book-containing-monthly-charts-of-stocks-i-noticed-that-stocks-that.md))

### Long-term flips: strategic, pension protocol, both accounts

The **long-term flip** is signalled by the QQQ weekly 10-week average crossing below (or back above) the 30-week average, and/or by the 30-week average itself beginning to curve downward. These are much rarer than short-term flips — occurring roughly once per multi-year cycle — and carry strategic weight. ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md), [WW 2010-07-19](../../raw/posts/2010-07-19-major-indexes-remain-in-long-term-down-trends-in-cash-or-short.md))

When a long-term flip threatens, Dr. Wish begins the **staged pension exit**: moving 25% of university retirement mutual fund assets to money-market at each subsequent signal. Trigger 1 = QQQ closes below 30-week average. Trigger 2 = 30-week begins to curve down. "If the market were to turn up in the next few months I would have no regrets — I would simply hop back on at a higher level." ([WW 2014-04-27](../../raw/posts/2014-04-27-i-do-not-want-to-be-long-in-this-market.md)) He also exits entirely from growth mutual fund accretions (not just new contributions) once a Stage 4 decline is confirmed. ([WW 2010-07-06](../../raw/posts/2010-07-06-at-the-beginning-of-a-big-market-decline-my-next-worden-webinar.md))

The long-term re-entry requires the 10-week average to cross back **above** the 30-week average and for the 30-week to curve up, confirming a Stage 2 up-trend. A bounce back to the 30-week in an otherwise declining market is not a re-entry signal — he has watched indexes rebound to kiss the 30-week only to decline again, and he does not take the bait. ([WW 2025-04-24](../../raw/posts/2025-04-24-blog-post-day-42-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-it-may-end-friday-however-longer-term-trends-remain-down-see-10.md), [WW 2009-06-14](../../raw/posts/2009-06-14-how-my-general-market-indicator-gmi-kept-me-and-my-401k-out-of-the-bear-market.md))

### The whipsaw toolkit

Several mechanisms prevent whipsaws from doing damage:

1. **Closing prices only.** He acts on daily or weekly closing prices, not intraday moves. A QQQ short-term up-trend begins the day QQQ *closes* above its 30-day average, not when it trades there intraday. ([WW 2013-10-20](../../raw/posts/2013-10-20-can-ta-protect-ourselves-from-a-1987-type-of-market-crash-speculative-bull-market-phase-beginning-tplm-green.md))
2. **Day 5 confirmation threshold.** "Once we pass [Day 5], trends tend to last for a while." Before that point, a reversal is likely and he does not scale into the new direction. ([WW 2010-05-09](../../raw/posts/2010-05-09-great-washington-worden-seminar-market-in-short-term-down-trend-mainly-in-cash.md))
3. **GMI two-consecutive-day rule.** A GMI buy signal requires two consecutive days above 3 before he commits. A single-day pop does not count. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
4. **Staged position sizing.** On Day 1, he buys a small amount. He adds more as the trend confirms — averaging up, never down. If stopped out by a whipsaw, he re-enters without ego when the signal re-fires. "There is plenty of room to make money in between [the top and the bottom]." ([WW 2020-03-15](../../raw/posts/2020-03-15-livermore-amputation-without-anaesthetics-after-avoiding-the-2000-decline-i-began-this-blog-in-2006-to-help-p.md))
5. **Accepting a higher re-entry price.** He explicitly prefers to miss the exact bottom and re-enter at a higher price once the new trend is confirmed, rather than try to call the turn and risk being wrong. ([WW 2025-04-24](../../raw/posts/2025-04-24-blog-post-day-42-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-it-may-end-friday-however-longer-term-trends-remain-down-see-10.md))

## 3. Long-term (30-week stage) transition table

These transitions are infrequent; each marks a potential regime shift. Transitions are: (a) QQQ weekly close **crosses below** the 30-week average; (b) the 30-week average **slope turns down** (confirming Stage 4 onset); (c) the 10-week average **crosses below** the 30-week average (Weinstein Stage-4 confirmation signal); and the mirror transitions back up. Sources drawn from blog posts; "no comment found" where no proximate post discusses the transition.

## 4. Detailed entries for consequential flips

### 2007–2008 — The Financial Crisis

The 2007 top was foreshadowed by T2108 reaching a peak of ~82% on 8 October 2007 ("about as high as it gets"), even as the GMI sat at 6. Dr. Wish's post that day was titled "GMI: 6; T2108 indicator at peak." One month later, on 8 November 2007, the title was "QQQQ breakdown coming" (GMI=5). By 9 November, his stance was "in cash." ([WW 2007-10-08](../../raw/posts/2007-10-08-gmi-6-t2108-indicator-at-peak.md), [WW 2007-11-08](../../raw/posts/2007-11-08-gmi-5-gmi-r-60-qqqq-breakdown-coming.md))

The GMI fell from 6 to 1–2 within days of the October 2007 top. Dr. Wish stayed defensive through most of 2008. By early June 2008, examining weakness in individual Dow-30 stocks, he moved **all pension money to a money-market fund**. By October 2008, the GMI and GMI-R were both at 0, T2108 had touched 6% (third-lowest reading since 1986), and the QQQQ short-term down-trend had reached Day 51. "The QQQQ has declined 33.9% since September 2" and "39% of Nasdaq-100 stocks have fallen 40–81% from September 2." ([WW 2008-10-06](../../raw/posts/2008-10-06-gmi-0-gmi-r-0-current-financial-mess-predicted-in-nyt-in-1999-t2108-at-extreme-low-6-my-gmi-indicators-kept-m.md), [WW 2008-11-12](../../raw/posts/2008-11-12-gmi-0-gmi-r-0-qqqq-short-term-down-trend-51-new-global-dow-index.md))

Re-entry came in April 2009 when the GMI hit 4 and the 10-week average crossed above the 30-week average — a confirmation of a tradeable advance. He moved 15% of pension from money market to growth mutual funds as a pilot buy, adding more as the advance confirmed. ([WW 2009-06-14](../../raw/posts/2009-06-14-how-my-general-market-indicator-gmi-kept-me-and-my-401k-out-of-the-bear-market.md))

What he said the driver was: his posts during 2007–2008 focus on indicator readings (GMI, T2108, stage analysis) rather than naming a macro catalyst. He acknowledged the financial crisis context in passing, noting an NYT prediction of the "current financial mess" from 1999. The signal was the indicators, not the news.

### 2011 — August Volatility (brief)

The GMI had been below 4 since July 27, 2011. On August 7 — Day 3 of a new QQQ short-term down-trend — Dr. Wish wrote "Crash coming?" He noted that T2108 was at 11% (below-20% territory but not yet at the single-digit extreme-buy zone of prior bear markets). His trading IRA and margin accounts were "almost 100% in cash." His university pension was still invested because he had recently triggered their market-timing rules. He wrote: "Down-trends often last for many days." ([WW 2011-08-07](../../raw/posts/2011-08-07-crash-coming-only-3rd-day-of-new-qqq-short-term-down-trend.md))

However, the weekly GMMA chart of QQQ showed the shorter-term averages (red) still above the longer-term averages (blue) — unlike 2008. He said the decline "looks more like the consolidation period we experienced in 2010. A major decline will have the red lines declining below the falling blue lines." This was an important example of using multiple timeframes to calibrate severity. The market did recover; T2108 reached a low around 7–8% in early October 2011, and the GMI returned to 6 by January 2012. No Stage 4 long-term flip was confirmed — the 30-week average never curved down.

This is also an example of the **pension timing-rule trap**: by trying to exit the pension too early during the preceding July mini-decline and then re-entering, he lost the ability to exit again without penalty when the August crash hit. The lesson he drew: "No one should stay in an investment vehicle that prohibits them from going to cash when they want."

### Head-fakes — three examples of short-lived down-trends

**June 7–12, 2022 (2-day up-trend then immediate reversal).** On June 7, 2022, Day 1 of a new up-trend (GMI=4, "nibbling"). Five calendar days later, June 12, Day 1 of a new down-trend — a 2-day up-trend that died immediately. He had taken only a small TQQQ position on Day 1 and did not scale in, so the loss was contained. This is exactly the pattern the 5-day confirmation threshold is designed to handle: "once we pass [Day 5], trends tend to last for a while."

**October 19–November 5, 2023 (6-day down-trend).** On October 11, Day 1 of a new up-trend. October 19 — only 6 trading days later — Day 1 of a new down-trend ("29 US new highs"). He had not yet scaled in heavily (the GMI had not clearly fired a Green signal). On November 5, the up-trend resumed. He re-entered without ego as the new signal fired, adding more as Days 5+ confirmed.

**January 7–9, 2024 (2-day down-trend).** On January 7, Day 1 down (after a 41-day up-trend). January 9 — 2 calendar days later — Day 1 up. He wrote on January 9: "buying TQQQ." Because the prior down-trend lasted only 2 days, he would not have added significantly to inverse positions, and the small initial position was quickly exited or expired. He re-entered the long side.

These head-fakes explain why Dr. Wish insists on the Day-5 threshold and the two-consecutive-day GMI rule: roughly 40% of new QQQ short-term down-trends end within 5 days, as he has published repeatedly since at least 2014.
