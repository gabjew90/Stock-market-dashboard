# Track record — calls & outcomes

Notable market calls Dr. Wish made on the blog and how they turned out.

**Data note:** this page draws on `raw/timeline.parquet`, built by `ww timeline` from 1,797 parsed daily-update posts (2005–2026). Of those, 989 rows are high-confidence (GMI value or QQQ day-count extracted); 808 are flagged (narrative-only or image-only posts where the parser could not extract a number). Individual calls below are cited to the specific posts from which the claim is drawn; the timeline dataset is referenced for aggregate patterns. Do not read the dataset entries as a complete audit trail — some posts are image-only or paywalled during archival, and the parser is conservative.

---

## 1994–2009 — Trading IRA up 1,200% (13×)

Writing in January 2009 at the depth of the financial crisis, Dr. Wish states that his trading IRA is up over **1,200% (13×)** since late 1994 — more than the top growth mutual fund tracked by IBD (up 382% over the same period). He escaped 2008 with only a −5% loss (vs indices −35–40%). The stated key: "I made money during the strong market rises and kept it, by being mainly out of the market during the major declines." "Conservation of capital during the inevitable market down-trends" is described as the lesson learned over 40+ years of investing. ([WW 2009-01-05](../../raw/posts/2009-01-05-im-up-1200-as-my-fellow-boomers-and-college-students-get-screwed-again-qqqq-in-17th-day-of-short-term-up-tren.md))

## 1995–2000 — IRA multiplied 14-fold

During the 1995–2000 Nasdaq bull run (index up approximately 584%), Dr. Wish multiplied his IRA **14-fold** using Darvas/O'Neil-inspired growth-stock techniques. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))

## 2000 — Exit on 30-week average reversal

Dr. Wish credits the reversal of the QQQQ's 30-week moving average downward in **2000** as the signal that took him out of the market before the dot-com crash. He describes himself (April 2005) as having "successfully dodged the 98 bear, the 2000–2002 bear and most of the present decline." ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))

**Outcome:** the Nasdaq Composite fell approximately 78% from its March 2000 peak to its October 2002 trough.

---

## 2003 — Re-entry on 30-week average reversal

The reversal of the QQQQ's 30-week average back upward in **2003** signalled re-entry. ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))

**Outcome:** the QQQQ roughly tripled from the 2003 bottom to the 2007 peak.

## October 2007 — T2108 peak warning at QQQ top

On **8 October 2007** — the day that proved to be very close to the bull-market top — Dr. Wish's daily post was titled "GMI: 6; T2108 indicator at peak." The GMI remained at 6 (maximum bullish), but he flagged that the Worden T2108 indicator was at 82%, "about as high as it gets," and that the QQQQ was in the **26th day of its short-term up-trend**. ([WW 2007-10-08](../../raw/posts/2007-10-08-gmi-6-t2108-indicator-at-peak.md))

Just one month later (8 November 2007) the GMI had dropped to 5 and his post was titled "QQQQ breakdown coming" as the trend deteriorated. By 9 November the stance had shifted to "in cash." ([WW 2007-11-08](../../raw/posts/2007-11-08-gmi-5-gmi-r-60-qqqq-breakdown-coming.md))

**Note:** the T2108 reading here is a warning signal, not a call to action — he did not exit at the 8 Oct top. The timeline data shows he moved to cash progressively across November as the GMI deteriorated.

## 2007–2008 — Navigating the financial crisis

The `raw/timeline.parquet` series shows Dr. Wish's GMI deteriorating sharply from late 2007 into 2008:

- November 2007: GMI dropped from 6 to 1–2 within days of the October top; stance shifted to "cash" repeatedly.
- Early January 2008: GMI at 0, "most new lows in 2 years, mostly in cash." He stayed defensive for much of the winter.
- The QQQQ down-trend stretched to 51 days by 12 November 2008 (GMI=0), during which he wrote that "the GMI has been 0 or 1 since September 2, during which the QQQQ has declined 33.9%" and that 39% of Nasdaq 100 stocks had fallen 40–81% from September 2. ([WW 2008-11-12](../../raw/posts/2008-11-12-gmi-0-gmi-r-0-qqqq-short-term-down-trend-51-new-global-dow-index.md))
- T2108 reached 2% on 9 October 2008 and 4% on 7 October 2008 — historically extreme oversold levels at the capitulation low.

**Outcome:** the S&P 500 bottomed in March 2009. The timeline shows the GMI turning back to 6 in late 2009, signalling re-entry.

## July 2010 — IRA track record updated to 14×

In a July 2010 self-introduction post, Dr. Wish updated his track record explicitly: the trading IRA is now up **14× (fourteen-fold)** since 1995. The 2009 post had stated 13× (1,200%). The increment reflects the gains made during the 2009–2010 recovery while pension funds stayed in cash through the 2008 bear. He describes keeping his university pension "safely in cash through all of the major market declines since 1998" and reinvesting in subsequent advances. ([WW 2010-07-19](../../raw/posts/2010-07-19-major-indexes-remain-in-long-term-down-trends-in-cash-or-short.md))

## February 2009 — Quantitative projection: Dow could reach 3,500

In a February 2009 post, Dr. Wish published a day-by-day comparison table of four bear markets (1929, 1973–74, 1987, 2008) from each bear's Dow peak. At day 260 post-peak in 2008, the decline matched the 1929 pace (both near −40%). If 2008 continued tracking 1929 minus approximately 14 percentage points, a Dow bottom around **3,500** was possible. At the post date (342 market days post-peak), the 1929 bear lasted 714 total trading days — suggesting 370+ more days of potential decline. GMI and GMI-R were both at zero; T2108 was at 13%. This was a quantitative scenario analysis, not a prediction. ([WW 2009-02-23](../../raw/posts/2009-02-23-comparison-of-current-bear-to-bear-markets-of-1929-1973-74-1987-suggests-dow-3500-possible.md))

**Outcome:** the Dow bottomed at approximately 6,547 in March 2009 — far above 3,500. The 2008 bear did not replay 1929's extended duration; the Fed and Treasury interventions produced a sharper, shorter bottom. This is an example of the bear-comparison framework being a useful framing tool even when the specific projection does not materialize.

## 2010 — Flash Crash brief signal failure

The timeline shows a GMI=0 reading on **15 June 2010** (28th day of a QQQ down-trend). This corresponds to the aftermath of the May 2010 Flash Crash. However, the GMI recovered quickly as markets rebounded, and the episode was relatively brief. The data confirms Dr. Wish maintained a cash stance during the worst of the mid-2010 decline but re-entered as the trend clarified.

## 2011 — August volatility

The timeline shows a GMI=0 / Sell reading on **15 December 2011**, with the GMI calling cash. This corresponds to the European sovereign debt crisis downturn in the second half of 2011. T2108 reached 7% in early October 2011 (an extreme oversold reading consistent with prior bottoms). The GMI returned to 6 by January 2012.

## 2016 January — World markets entering BWR

On **10 January 2016**, Dr. Wish published a post titled "All World Stock Markets entering BWR Down-trends! I am in cash and monitoring T2108." He examined 37 country ETFs and found that — with the exceptions of Belgium and Ireland — all were in established BWR down-trends. The GMI had gone to 0 on 5 January 2016. He linked the T2108 indicator (then at 15%) to potential contrarian bottom signals and set his threshold at single-digit T2108 for a potential re-entry. ([WW 2016-01-10](../../raw/posts/2016-01-10-all-world-stock-markets-entering-bwr-down-trends-i-am-in-cash-and-scared-and-monitoring-t2108.md))

**Outcome:** the S&P 500 bottomed on 11 February 2016. The timeline shows the GMI recovering to 3 by 24 February 2016, signalling the start of a new short-term up-trend. The decline was sharp but relatively brief — SPY fell approximately 12% peak-to-trough before recovering.

## 2018 Q4 — Cash at GMI=0 / T2108=5.65%

By **23 December 2018**, with QQQ in a deep down-trend, Dr. Wish had the GMI at 0 and the T2108 at 5.65% — among the most extreme oversold readings in the indicator's history going back to 1986. He wrote: "with the GMI at 0 (of 6) and still on a Red signal, it is time for me to remain in cash." Only 62 US stocks (of 4,929) had both daily and weekly RWB patterns. ([WW 2018-12-23](../../raw/posts/2018-12-23-time-for-cash.md))

The timeline confirms GMI remained at 0 / Red through the end of 2018 before recovering in January 2019.

**Outcome:** QQQ bottomed on 24 December 2018 and rallied sharply in early 2019. By 6 January 2019 the GMI had ticked back to 0 but with an "invested" stance noted — a signal he was beginning to look for re-entry.

## February–March 2020 — COVID exit to 100% cash before the crash

**The call:** On **15 March 2020** — near the trough of the COVID crash — Dr. Wish published a post titled "Livermore: Amputation without anaesthetics." He disclosed that the GMI had gone **Red on 26 February 2020** and that he had been **100% in cash for weeks before that** signal, having identified warning signs ahead of the official GMI flip. He described SPY as being in "a clear Stage 4 decline" on the weekly chart. He explicitly stated: "I do not care if I miss the bottom or the top" and wrote that for tax-deferred accounts he had moved entirely to money market funds. ([WW 2020-03-15](../../raw/posts/2020-03-15-livermore-amputation-without-anaesthetics-after-avoiding-the-2000-decline-i-began-this-blog-in-2006-to-help-p.md))

**Signal used:** GMI going Red + weekly Stage 4 identification on SPY. He also noted that T2108 had hit **3%** by mid-March — an extreme oversold reading consistent with capitulation.

**Livermore quote used:** "It never was my thinking that made the big money for me. It always was my sitting."

**Outcome:** SPY bottomed on 23 March 2020. The COVID crash was among the fastest bear-market declines in history (-34% from peak to trough in about 33 days). Being 100% cash weeks before the GMI Red signal — and before the March decline accelerated — preserved capital through the sharp leg down.

## November 2021 — Exit at QQQ peak on breadth divergence

**The call:** In November 2021, while QQQ was still at all-time highs (Day U-26 of the short-term up-trend), Dr. Wish noticed that new lows in his US stock universe were surging. On **22 November 2021** — which proved to be QQQ's exact peak — he recorded 438 new lows while QQQ was at an ATH. He exited the market. ([WW 2023-06-19](../../raw/posts/2023-06-19-blog-post-here-is-an-explanation-of-how-i-compute-new-us-highs-and-lows-each-day-and-used-it-to-exit-in-11-20.md))

**Signal used:** breadth divergence — surging new lows while the index was at highs. Not the GMI (which can remain GREEN longer). He describes this as a "lucky call" but attributes it to the divergence signal.

**Outcome:** QQQ entered a Stage 4 decline after the November 2021 peak. He stayed out of the market for over a year.

## 2022 — Extended bear year (GMI=0 multiple episodes)

The `raw/timeline.parquet` data shows multiple GMI=0 episodes through 2022, consistent with a full bear year:

- February 2022: GMI=0 / Red / stance=cash (early warning as rate hike fears built).
- May 2022 (Day 23–28 of QQQ down-trend): GMI=0. In a 22 May post, Dr. Wish wrote "I got out of the market in November and I do not have to try to guess the bottom." ([WW 2022-05-22](../../raw/posts/2022-05-22-blog-post-day-28-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-gmi0-and-red-i-exited-in-november-and-do-not-have-to-catch-the.md))
- September–October 2022: GMI=0 / Red on multiple readings as the ultimate bear-market low approached.

**Outcome:** QQQ bottomed in October 2022. The timeline shows the GMI recovering through late 2022 and reaching 3 / Green in November 2022 as the recovery began.

## March–April 2025 — IWM Stage 4 call and exit to cash

On **30 March 2025**, Dr. Wish published a post stating that IWM "looks like it is at the BEGINNING of a Stage 4 down-trend" and that he had transferred his pension mutual fund accumulations out of equities. He cited the GMI (Red since 24 February 2025), the 10-week average crossing below the 30-week average on the IWM weekly chart, and T2108 at 24% (not yet at a panic bottom). He drew a direct parallel to the 2022 decline and said he would wait on the sidelines for the 30-week average to curve back up. ([WW 2025-03-30](../../raw/posts/2025-03-30-blog-post-day-24-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-iwm-looks-like-it-is-at-the-beginning-of-a-stage-4-down-trend-s.md))

The timeline confirms GMI=0 / Red readings in January and March 2025, with stance=cash by early March.

**Outcome (as of May 2026):** After the March 2025 tariff-related decline, the QQQ short-term trend recovered. By 3–5 May 2026 the timeline shows GMI=6 / Green with an up-trend in progress (Day 17–19 of QQQ short-term up-trend). The 2025 Stage 4 call for IWM was timely — IWM underperformed QQQ through the decline phase.

## 2026 Q1 — March tariff-shock exit to cash

The timeline shows Dr. Wish moving back to cash by early March 2026. On **8 March 2026**: GMI=0 / Red / stance=cash — "Day 22 of QQQ short-term down-trend." On **22 March 2026**: GMI=0 / Red / cash — "Day 32 of QQQ short-term down-trend; QQQ 10-week average about to cross below 30-week average." By **31 March 2026** the QQQ down-trend had reached Day 39. The GMI recovered sharply to 6 / Green by **3 May 2026** (Day 17 of a new up-trend), with the stance back to "invested" by 5 May 2026.

## Aggregate patterns from the timeline dataset (2005–2026)

Based on 939 daily-update posts with a parseable GMI value (out of 1,797 total, 2005–2026):

- **GMI=6 (maximum bull):** 325 readings — the most common single value, reflecting bull-market duration dominance.
- **GMI=0 (maximum bear):** 29 readings — concentrated in late 2007–2008 (financial crisis), 2011 (European debt crisis), 2015–2016 (oil/China downturn), 2018 Q4, 2021–2022 bear, 2025–2026 tariff downturn.
- **GMI state = Green:** 160 readings; **Buy:** 108; **Sell:** 97; **Red:** 80. (State was extracted from posts that explicitly wrote "Green," "Buy," "Sell," or "Red" alongside the GMI — not all posts include this label.)
- **Stance = cash:** 321 posts; **invested:** 167; **cautious:** 96. Cash is the most common explicit stance, consistent with his posture as a defensive trader who moves to cash rather than fighting down-trends.
- **QQQ trend data (93 posts with day-count):** longest down-trend logged: **Day 54** (17 November 2008, financial crisis); longest up-trend logged: **Day 83** (15 December 2006, the 2006–2007 bull run). Up-trends outnumber down-trends in the dataset (53 up vs. 40 down), consistent with the secular bull market dominating the period.
- **T2108 extremes:** highest parsed reading: 92% (31 October 2011, a false dawn in the European crisis recovery); lowest: 2% (9 October 2008, very near the financial crisis capitulation low).

**Caveat:** 808 of 1,797 daily-update rows are "flagged" (parser found no GMI value or QQQ day-count — the posts are image-heavy, chart-only, or use novel phrasing not captured by the current regex). The patterns above reflect the 55% that were machine-readable; the remaining 45% may shift these distributions when a future human or LLM pass tags them.
