# WW 2017-12-17 — A strategy for deciding when to sell stocks; GDS, NVDA

**Source:** [A strategy for decidng when to sell stocks; $GDS, $NVDA](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/12/a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish describes his shift to **daily RWB charts** as the primary tool for both entry timing and exits. He had previously used the weekly yellowband up-trend with the 10-week average as the exit signal, but found that a weekly close below the 10-week average could give back too much profit. The daily chart offers a faster, more precise sell signal. He also introduces a new TC2000 scan ("12162017DailyRWBBounce") that finds RWB stocks that recently dipped below all six red lines and have now recovered above them, and illustrates the system on GDS (a Chinese tech IPO) and NVDA.

## Key claims

- The exit rule on a daily RWB chart: hold while the stock closes above all six red lines; sell (exit) if it **closes below all six red lines**. ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- For a large profit, he suggests waiting for **two consecutive closes below all red lines** before selling, to avoid a single-day whipsaw. ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- When the red lines **converge** (compress together), it signals the stock is going sideways or losing momentum. The right action at that point is to wait for a breakout in either direction rather than anticipate. "React, do not anticipate." ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- Entry criteria on this scan: stock must have recently closed below all red lines (oversold dip), then recovered above all red lines in an RWB pattern. The lowest red line value shown on the chart (the 15-day EMA) is a specific dollar-level stop. ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- GLB stock GDS: weekly GLB in September 2016 on the highest volume since IPO; it remained above all red lines for 3 months before its first close below all red lines, having nearly doubled. ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- Scan ran on 4,900+ stocks, yielded 13 candidates meeting criteria (all in RWB up-trend, recent dip below all reds, now recovered). ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
- Stocks **not above their last green line top** are disqualified despite appearing in the scan, due to overhead supply. ([WW 2017-12-17](../../raw/posts/2017-12-17-a-strategy-for-decidng-when-to-sell-stocks-gds-nvda.md))
