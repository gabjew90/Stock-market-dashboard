# WW 2017-01-29 — David Ryan quote; ATH-past-40-days + lower BB scan; PLAY

**Source:** [On David Ryan and my new #TC2000 scan for GLB rockets bouncing off up of support: $PLAY](../../raw/posts/2017-01-29-on-david-ryan-and-my-new-tc2000-scan-for-glb-rockets-bouncing-off-up-of-support-play.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/01/on-david-ryan-and-my-new-tc2000-scan-for-glb-rockets-bouncing-off-up-of-support-play/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish quotes David Ryan (William O'Neil's protege) from _Momentum Masters_ (2015) on simplifying stock selection to two setups: breakouts and pullbacks. He shows how this aligns with his own GLB framework. The post introduces a new TC2000 scan (name: `01292017ATHhipast40daysBLBB`) that combines two conditions: stocks in his ~800-stock ATH watchlist that hit a new all-time high in the past 40 days AND are currently bouncing up from their lower Bollinger Band (15.2). The scan is distinct from the BOS scan (which uses a broader universe and a different trigger) and complements the GLB alert by finding pullback re-entries after a prior breakout.

## Key claims

- David Ryan quote (verbatim from _Momentum Masters_ p. 72): "I basically simplify it down to two, breakouts and pullbacks… You just have to draw a line across the top of where most of the stock's trading has taken place. Then you buy as it moves through that line… Buying pullbacks are a bit more complicated but offer another entry point to get aboard a leading stock." ([WW 2017-01-29](../../raw/posts/2017-01-29-on-david-ryan-and-my-new-tc2000-scan-for-glb-rockets-bouncing-off-up-of-support-play.md))
- New scan `01292017ATHhipast40daysBLBB`: all stocks in the ATH watchlist (~800 stocks) that (1) hit a new all-time high in the past 40 days AND (2) are currently bouncing from their lower Bollinger Band (15.2). Finds post-GLB pullback entries.
- A pullback/bounce entry **does not require volume** (unlike a GLB breakout). Low volume on the pullback is a positive sign that selling has dried up.
- Sell stop on a bounce entry: placed just below the recent bounce/support low.
- PLAY example: gapped up on unusual volume in December 2016, traded sideways, then bounced off lower BB; earnings up +127% prior quarter, IBD composite 93, EPS 99, short ratio 7.2, up 47% over the year.
- GMI at 6 of 6 at the time of the post (strong buy environment).
