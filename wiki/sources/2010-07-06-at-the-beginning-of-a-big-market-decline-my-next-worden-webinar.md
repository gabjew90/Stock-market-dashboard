# WW 2010-07-06 — Pension exit discipline; topping-pattern PCF scan; GMI=0

**Source:** [At the BEGINNING of a big market decline? My next Worden webinar](../../raw/posts/2010-07-06-at-the-beginning-of-a-big-market-decline-my-next-worden-webinar.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2010/07/at-the-beginning-of-a-big-market-decline-my-next-worden-webinar/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish explains his decision in July 2010 to move 60% of his pension from stock mutual funds to money market funds, despite the market having already declined significantly. He describes a TC2007 PCF (personal criteria formula) that detects stocks and ETFs whose long-term average is *just beginning* to reverse down — the same scan that had flagged Enron before it collapsed. He uses this scan on all ETFs and finds 91 of 951 showing early topping patterns across world markets. The GMI and GMI-R are both at zero.

## Key claims

- **Topping-pattern PCF:** Dr. Wish's TC2007 PCF finds patterns where "the long term average is just beginning to reverse down" — distinguishing these from stocks already in established down-trends. It flagged Enron before news emerged, and in July 2010 flagged 91 ETFs including IVV (S&P 500), IYJ (Dow 30), plus most emerging markets. ([WW 2010-07-06](../../raw/posts/2010-07-06-at-the-beginning-of-a-big-market-decline-my-next-worden-webinar.md))
- **Psychology of late exit:** "She was incredulous that I would do so after the market was already down so much." Dr. Wish argues that the weekly trend, not the recent decline, is the right signal — if the 30-week average is declining, the trend is still down.
- **Pension exit principle:** "When the weekly averages tend to represent trends that last for months, I have plenty of time to ride a new up-trend." Getting out late is better than riding the full decline; he would re-enter at a higher price if the market recovered quickly with no regrets.
- **2010 context:** GMI=0, GMI-R=0; T2108 at 25% (not yet oversold enough for a bottom); QQQQ in day 6 of short-term down-trend; QQQQ and SPY below 10-week avg for 9 weeks; only 8% of Nasdaq100 stocks with MACD above signal line.
- His early exits in 2000–2002 and 2008 are cited as validation of the approach.
