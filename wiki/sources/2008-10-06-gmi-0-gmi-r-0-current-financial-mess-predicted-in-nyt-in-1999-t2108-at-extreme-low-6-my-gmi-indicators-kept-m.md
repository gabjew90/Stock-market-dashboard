# WW 2008-10-06 — GMI: 0; T2108 at extreme low 6%; GMI indicators kept me out

**Source:** [GMI: 0; GMI-R: 0; T2108 at extreme low: 6%; My GMI indicators kept me out of this decline](../../raw/posts/2008-10-06-gmi-0-gmi-r-0-current-financial-mess-predicted-in-nyt-in-1999-t2108-at-extreme-low-6-my-gmi-indicators-kept-m.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2008/10/gmi-0-gmi-r-0-current-financial-mess-predicted-in-nyt-in-1999-t2108-at-extreme-low-6-my-gmi-indicators-kept-me-out-of-this-decline-how-about-you/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written during the acute phase of the 2008 financial crisis (early October 2008, when T2108 hit 6%), this post is Dr. Wish's real-time account of having successfully avoided the decline. GMI and GMI-R were at 0. He put his 401K pension into a money market fund in June 2008 after seeing weakness in the Dow 30. He provides T2108 historical context: going back to 1986, only twice had T2108 gone below the October 2008 reading of 6% — October 1987 (0.5%) and August 1990 (5.3%). All other market bottoms occurred at 7% or above. He frames the 2008 decline as relatively slow compared to 1929 (Dow -13.5% in a single day, Oct 28, 1929).

## Key claims

- T2108 at 6% in October 2008 was historically extreme: only October 1987 (0.5%) and August 1990 (5.3%) went lower among all market bottoms back to 1986.
- The 777-point Dow drop on September 29, 2008 was only 6.98% — modest versus October 1987 (-22.6% in one day) or 1929 (-13.5%, -11.7% on consecutive days).
- By this date the Dow was down ~26% from its October 2007 peak — "relatively slow and small — at least thus far."
- On June 8, 2008 Dr. Wish transferred all pension money from mutual funds into money market funds after examining Dow 30 stock weakness.
- With GMI=0, he prefers to wait for a new up-trend rather than guessing the bottom; T2108 at extreme lows is not itself sufficient to go long in individual stocks.
- He started the blog in 2005 specifically because he was upset by media pundits who kept people in stocks through the 2000–2002 decline.
