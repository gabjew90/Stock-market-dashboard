# WW 2022-04-17 — WeeklyGreenBar scan detailed; green-bar trailing stop rule

**Source:** [Blog Post: $QQQ and $SPY closed back below their 10 week averages; GMI remains Red; Cash is king but there are 25 stocks at ATHs that passed my WeeklyGreenBar scan](../../raw/posts/2022-04-17-blog-post-qqq-and-spy-closed-back-below-their-10-week-averages-gmi-remains-red-cash-is-king-but-there-are-25.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2022/04/blog-post-qqq-and-spy-closed-back-below-their-10-week-averages-gmi-remains-red-cash-is-king-but-there-are-25-stocks-trading-at-aths-that-passed-my-weeklygreenbar-scan-sre-nem-i-describe-scan/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish describes the WeeklyGreenBar (WGB) scan in full detail, using SRE as a worked example. He explains the green-bar definition, the required moving-average alignment, the stop-loss placement, and the exit rules. He also explains why stocks at all-time highs in commodities/defense can still produce green-bar signals during a GMI Red period. SRE is not a recommendation — used for illustration only.

## Key claims

- **WeeklyGreenBar scan criteria (explicit):** The stock must (1) be in a 4wk>10wk>30wk MA alignment (Stage 2), (2) have bounced off the rising 4-week average last week, (3) closed the week *higher than the week before* (this is the "green bar" — a week that does not close higher is not green), (4) have RS vs S&P 500 at a 20-week high last week, (5) hit an ATH last week. Candidate pool: Dr. Wish's IBD/MarketSmith watchlist (stocks recently mentioned by those services). ([WW 2022-04-17](../../raw/posts/2022-04-17-blog-post-qqq-and-spy-closed-back-below-their-10-week-averages-gmi-remains-red-cash-is-king-but-there-are-25.md))
- **Trailing stop rule:** "An advancing stock rides its rising 4 wk avg for many weeks." Lighten or sell when the stock *ends a week below its 4-week avg*. Final exit: close below the 10-week avg. Can also trail stop at each subsequent green bar's low.
- **Initial stop:** sell if stock trades back below last week's low.
- Green-bar signals work even during a GMI Red period if a subsector (oil, defense, commodities) is in Stage 2. But context matters — GMI Green is the preferred background.
- In April 2022, 25 stocks from the watchlist passed the WGB scan despite the broader market being in a Stage 4 decline; almost all were commodity/defense-related.
- GLB is an additional plus but not required for the WGB scan.
