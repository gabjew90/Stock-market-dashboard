## What it covers

The origin story and detailed rationale of the GLB strategy. Dr. Wish traces it to his observation in the 1960s of a monthly chart book — stocks that reached an ATH, declined for a few months, then went to a new ATH were often big gainers. He formally explains the overhead-supply mechanism (why a stock breaking through a prior ATH is significant), how to find GLBs in TC2000, and provides 6 recent examples: JMIA, GM, PGNY, KC, MGNI, CSIQ. Includes a clear rule for IPO GLBs and the re-buy rule for stocks that are not at ATH.

## Key claims

- GLB origin: monthly chart books in the 1960s showed ATH → consolidation → new ATH = often big gainer. Dr. Wish says he "created my green line break-out (GLB) strategy many years later" based on this observation.
- **Overhead supply mechanism:** people who bought near the prior ATH (peak) only want to break even — they sell as the stock rises back toward their purchase price. This constitutes "overhead supply or selling pressure all the way back up to the green line." If the stock overcomes all that supply and breaks out, it shows "considerable buying interest and technical strength."
- GLB is "most significant if it occurs on above average daily trading volume."
- GLB definition: "the first time the equity finally *closes* above the green line" — intraday trading above is insufficient.
- IPO GLB adjustment: for very recent IPOs, he might draw the green line "after only 1-2 months of consolidation" instead of 3 months.
- How to find them in TC2000: scan for 52-week high (`H=maxh52` weekly or `h=maxh250` daily); review monthly charts; right-click to set an alert (valid 1 year) for any bar that trades above the line.
- Buying rule for stocks not yet at a GLB: "buy only stocks in an up-trend that are trading above their last green line top if they missed the break-out." (Do not buy stocks below their most recent ATH — overhead supply exists.)
- He does NOT set a hard stop loss for a GLB, to avoid intraday whipsaws. He monitors near the close each day and exits if it looks like the stock will close below the green line.
- "Most great traders... are wrong 50% or more of the time but succeed because they sell their losses quickly while they are small and let their gains grow larger."
- Six concurrent examples (January 2021): JMIA, GM, PGNY, KC, MGNI, CSIQ — all with charts.
