# WW 2024-06-16 — x8/x21/30-day moving average alignment as bounce entry setup

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 29 of $QQQ short term up-trend; Introducing the x8/x21/30 day set-up, examples: $COST, $ANF, $NVDA, $CVLT](../../raw/posts/2024-06-16-blog-post-day-29-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-introducing-the-x8-x21-30-day-set-up-examplescost-anf-nvda-cvlt.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2024/06/blog-post-day-29-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-introducing-the-x8-x21-30-day-set-up-examplescost-anf-nvda-cvlt/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Introduced in June 2024, the x8/x21/30-day setup is a daily-chart bounce-off-support entry. For advancing stocks, the alignment x8 (8-day exponential average) > x21 (21-day exponential average) > 30-day simple average is the structural requirement. The entry trigger: buy a stock bouncing up off its 8-day EMA. The exit: sell if/when the stock closes below the 8-day EMA. TC2000 scan criteria for finding candidates: stocks with the above alignment, up at least 50% from a year ago, hit an ATH within the last 5 weeks, and currently bouncing off the x8. Fundamentals are checked via MarketSmith before buying. COST, ANF, NVDA, and CVLT are shown as worked examples. The 8-day EMA influence comes from Pat Walker; the 21-day EMA from IBD/CAN SLIM research. Dr. Wish credits both but describes the combined x8/x21/30 setup as his own construction.

## Key claims

- x8 EMA > x21 EMA > 30-day simple average = bullish daily-chart alignment for an advancing stock.
- Entry: buy when a stock bouncing up off its 8-day EMA; exit: sell if it closes back below the 8-day EMA.
- TC2000 scan: alignment present + up 50%+ YoY + ATH within 5 weeks + bouncing off x8.
- The setup's value is defining an immediate-failure exit (close below x8) which enforces small losses.
- Fundamentals check (MarketSmith) required before committing capital.
