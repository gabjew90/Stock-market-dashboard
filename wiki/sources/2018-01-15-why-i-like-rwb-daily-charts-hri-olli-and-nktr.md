# WW 2018-01-15 — RWB daily bounce scan: entry rule, stop calculation, and exit rule

**Source:** [Why I like RWB daily charts — $HRI, $OLLI and $NKTR](../../raw/posts/2018-01-15-why-i-like-rwb-daily-charts-hri-olli-and-nktr.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2018/01/why-i-like-rwb-daily-charts-hri-olli-and-nktr/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A detailed explanation of the daily RWB bounce-entry scan (TC2000 scan named "12252017BounceRWBdaily"). The key entry signal is a stock that recently closed below all six red lines in the daily RWB pattern and has now closed back above all of them — signaling a resumption of the advance. The post also formalizes the stop-calculation method: the dollar value of the lowest red line (the 15-day EMA) is shown on the chart, and the distance between current price and that value is the approximate dollar risk if the trade fails. HRI, OLLI, and NKTR are concrete examples.

## Key claims

- Daily RWB chart has 13 daily exponential MAs: 6 red (shorter-term), 6 blue (longer-term), plus a gray dotted line (daily closes).
- **Bounce entry signal:** find an RWB stock where the daily close recently fell below all six rising red lines, then closed back above all of them. This identifies "the stock is in a confirmed RWB up-trend, pulled back to an oversold level, and has just resumed the advance."
- TC2000 scan: named "12252017BounceRWBdaily" — available free to TC2000 users in the Dr. Wish club.
- **Stop calculation using the lowest red line:** the 15-day exponential average (the lowest red line) is shown in large digits on the chart. The stop is set just below this level. The dollar distance = (current price − lowest red line value) = approximate maximum loss if bought at market and stopped out. Example: HRI at 65.62, lowest red line at 63.13 → maximum loss of ~$2.49 per share if stopped just below 63.13.
- **Exit rule:** "If a stock closes back below all of its red lines for 1 or 2 days, I consider the break-out to have failed and I exit." (Slightly different from the single-close rule in other posts — here 1 or 2 days gives a bit more room before exiting. He is not fully consistent on 1 vs. 2 closes below all red lines.)
- Additional observation: "My biggest problem is selling a rising stock too soon. I get scared out when it or the market zigs down." An analysis of his recent sales showed that waiting until the stock closes below all red lines for 2 days would have kept him in stocks that kept rising.
- HRI quarterly earnings = +309% — he likes good fundamentals as well as technicals. IBD composite rating confirms candidate quality.
