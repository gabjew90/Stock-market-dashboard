# WW 2021-01-18 — IBD/MS growth stocks beat TQQQ for the first time; leadership shift

**Source:** [Blog post: For the first time many individual stocks outperformed just holding $TQQQ during a $QQQ short term up-trend](../../raw/posts/2021-01-18-blog-post-for-the-first-time-many-individual-stocks-outperformed-just-holding-tqqq-during-a-qqq-short-term-up.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2021/01/blog-post-for-the-first-time-many-individual-stocks-outperformed-just-holding-tqqq-during-a-qqq-short-term-up-trend-ibd-marketsmith-growth-stocks-did-best-while-faang-stocks-faltered-qqq-on-sup/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish documents an unusual finding: for the first time in his tracking, a large proportion of individual growth stocks beat TQQQ during a QQQ short-term up-trend (11/5/2020 to 1/15/2021). His IBD/MarketSmith Growth Stock watchlist had a median gain of +24%, with the top 10 advancing +125%–+386%. TQQQ gained +17.6%. He interprets this as a signal to focus on growth stocks when market conditions are unusually strong, while managing risk with stops and position size. He also flags FAANG underperformance as a potential warning sign.

## Key claims

- From 11/5/20 to 1/15/21: TQQQ gained +17.6%; QQQ +5.95%. Typically TQQQ beats 90%+ of individual stocks.
- **Exception:** 60% of IBD/MarketSmith growth stocks beat TQQQ. Median gain for the growth watchlist: +24%. Top performers: +125% to +386%.
- Comparison: only 25% of Nasdaq100 stocks and 38% of S&P500 stocks beat TQQQ — still worse than growth stocks.
- FAANG stocks *declined* during this up-trend period (except AAPL). This divergence, combined with 63.7% bullish advisors, raises a warning signal: "market leaders top before the general market does."
- His conclusion for the current environment: focus on promising growth stocks, manage risk with stops and position size.
- QQQ sitting at the bottom of its up-trend channel, below its rising 4-week average for the first time in 10 weeks — a signal to monitor.
- GMI=6, GMI2=4 (short-term weakness in the more sensitive indicator). ([WW 2021-01-18](../../raw/posts/2021-01-18-blog-post-for-the-first-time-many-individual-stocks-outperformed-just-holding-tqqq-during-a-qqq-short-term-up.md))
