# WW 2020-03-15 — Livermore's 'amputation': 2020 exit and re-entry philosophy

**Source:** [Livermore: Amputation without anaesthetics — After avoiding the 2000 decline, I began this blog in 2006 to help people time the market](../../raw/posts/2020-03-15-livermore-amputation-without-anaesthetics-after-avoiding-the-2000-decline-i-began-this-blog-in-2006-to-help-p.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2020/03/livermore-amputation-without-anaesthetics-after-avoiding-the-2000-decline-i-began-this-blog-in-2006-to-help-p/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A real-time account written at the start of the March 2020 COVID crash (GMI=0, Red signal active since February 26, 2020). Dr. Wish explains his exit methodology from a philosophical level: watch what the market is doing, not what pundits say; use simple GMI criteria; go to cash gradually when indicators flash Red; re-enter only after indicators confirm a new up-trend. He includes the Livermore "amputation without anaesthetics" quote about those who couldn't take small losses facing catastrophic losses. He identifies the SPY as likely entering a Weinstein Stage 4, comparing the setup to prior bear markets.

## Key claims

- "If it is impossible to time the market, then why have I been out of the market in bad times and back in during the good times? Just luck?"
- The GMI=0 since February 26, 2020 was the signal. He had been 100% cash for weeks before this post.
- His re-entry philosophy: "I do not care if I miss the bottom or the top. There is plenty of room to make money in between." He enters *after* indicators confirm a new up-trend, gradually, at ever-higher prices.
- All persons with tax-deferred IRAs/401(k) accounts can exit and re-enter (transfer to money market, then back) without tax consequences — "I know some employer plans limit market timing, but they usually let one exit and enter as long as it does not occur too often."
- Livermore quote (October 1907): "There came the awful day of reckoning for the bulls... who, dreading the pain of a small loss at the beginning, were now about to suffer total amputation — without anaesthetics."
- SPY weekly chart: only three weeks below the 30-week average (solid red line), but large weekly selling volume in two of the last three weeks. "We are at the *beginning*, not the end of a Weinstein Stage IV decline. Bear markets often last 10 months or more."
- T2108=3% at the time — historically extreme, but Dr. Wish warns this doesn't mean a bottom is imminent.
