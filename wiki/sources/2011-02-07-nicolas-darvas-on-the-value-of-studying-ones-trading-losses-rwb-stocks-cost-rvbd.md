# WW 2011-02-07 — Darvas/Neill on studying losses; RWB stocks; IBD as required reading

**Source:** [Nicolas Darvas, on the value of studying one's trading losses; RWB stocks, COST, RVBD](../../raw/posts/2011-02-07-nicolas-darvas-on-the-value-of-studying-ones-trading-losses-rwb-stocks-cost-rvbd.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2011/02/nicolas-darvas-on-the-value-of-studying-ones-trading-losses-rwb-stocks-cost-rvbd/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish draws on Darvas, Neill (Tape Reading & Market Tactics, 1931), and his own classroom exercise to argue that studying trading losses is a prerequisite for improvement. He then applies his market indicators (GMI, GMI-R at max) and shows two RWB stocks: COST (approaching its ATH at $75.23 from the weekly GMMA chart) and RVBD (bouncing off support, stop below 30-day avg at ~$34.95). IBD is described as essential for stock selection — he credits it for turning his trading around in the 1980s.

## Key claims

- "Every great trader... urges us to study our losses." Darvas paid tuition on losses; Neill dedicated his book "to my losses." Dr. Wish reverses his rules after printing and reviewing his losing trade charts.
- His classroom exercise: students trade $100,000 in simulation for 9 weeks, then analyze losses to revise their entry/exit rules.
- **IBD requirement:** "All of my students are required to get a student subscription to IBD and to read the newspaper daily... I never consistently made money trading until I started reading IBD in the 1980's." IBD50 list is the primary stock-selection pool. ([WW 2011-02-07](../../raw/posts/2011-02-07-nicolas-darvas-on-the-value-of-studying-ones-trading-losses-rwb-stocks-cost-rvbd.md))
- **Stop on RWB bounce trade:** RVBD bouncing from support → stop loss below its 30-day average (~$34.95). This is an early documented use of the 30-day average as a stop level on individual stocks.
- GMI and GMI-R at maximum values; 79% successful 10-day new highs; QQQ in day 53 of short-term up-trend.
- COST approaching its ATH at $75.23 — "a *close* above $75.23 could be a sign of considerable strength." Green line (ATH level) established.
