# WW 2026-02-16 — QQQ on green line top and 30-week average; Stage 4 warning

**Source:** [Blog: Day 8 of $QQQ short term down-trend; Utilities had most ATHs; Rotation away from tech to energy; QQQ on Green Line top and 30-week average](../../raw/posts/2026-02-16-blog-day-8-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-utilities-had-most-aths-13-see-list-followed-by-oilgas-10-rotation-aw.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2026/02/blog-day-8-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-utilities-had-most-aths-13-see-list-followed-by-oilgas-10-rotation-aw/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A February 2026 warning post. QQQ had pulled back to sit exactly on its prior green line top (~599.35) and its 30-week average simultaneously — a structural level that, if broken on a close below a declining 30-week average, would signal a Weinstein Stage 4 decline. Dr. Wish notes sector rotation away from tech and growth: utilities accounted for 21% of the 61 US stocks hitting ATHs that week; energy stocks were second. The GMI was still Green but weakening. The weekly 10.4 stochastic for QQQ had fallen to 51 — in a strong up-trend it stays above 80 for months. The 4-week average had crossed below the 10-week average, another sign of weakening.

## Key claims

- When QQQ closes at its prior green line top AND at its 30-week average simultaneously, it is at a critical structural level: a confirmed breakdown there historically triggered major Stage 4 declines.
- The 30-week average curving down is the key final trigger to exit the market entirely — same rule as every prior decline since 2000.
- A 10.4 weekly stochastic reading of 51 signals waning momentum; in confirmed up-trends it stays above 80 for months.
- Sector rotation to defensive sectors (utilities, energy) while tech weakens is an early warning indicator alongside the GMI.
- The 4wk average crossing below the 10wk average is a weakening signal even before the GMI turns Red.
