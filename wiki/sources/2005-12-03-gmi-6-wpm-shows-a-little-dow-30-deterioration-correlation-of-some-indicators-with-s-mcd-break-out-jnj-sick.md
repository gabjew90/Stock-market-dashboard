# WW 2005-12-03 — Early GMI breadth detail; short-term trend indicator vs 10wk breadth; MCD trade

**Source:** [GMI: +6; WPM shows a little DOW 30 deterioration; correlation of some indicators with S&P 500; MCD break-out; JNJ sick?](../../raw/posts/2005-12-03-gmi-6-wpm-shows-a-little-dow-30-deterioration-correlation-of-some-indicators-with-s-mcd-break-out-jnj-sick.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/12/gmi-6-wpm-shows-a-little-dow-30-deterioration-correlation-of-some-indicators-with-s-mcd-break-out-jnj-sick/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

An early (December 2005) detailed methodological post showing two GMI-adjacent breadth indicators: (1) percentage of stocks above their 10-week average, and (2) percentage of stocks in a short-term up-trend (defined as "10-day average above a rising 30-day average"). He charts both against the S&P 500, identifying a meaningful divergence at the October bottom: the 10-week breadth fell but short-term trend breadth rose — potentially a useful signal. He also discusses the WPM (Worden P/E Model or a related market strength table), the QQQQ up-trend in day 23, and a MCD breakout trade he entered.

## Key claims

- **Short-term up-trend definition (breadth computation):** A stock is in a short-term up-trend when its "10 day average is above a rising 30 day average." He computes this for all ~4,000 stocks in his universe. On this date: 65% in short-term up-trend, 73% above 10-week avg. ([WW 2005-12-03](../../raw/posts/2005-12-03-gmi-6-wpm-shows-a-little-dow-30-deterioration-correlation-of-some-indicators-with-s-mcd-break-out-jnj-sick.md))
- **Divergence signal:** At the October 2005 bottom retest, the % above 10wk avg fell from 36% to 24% while % in short-term up-trend *rose* from 14% to 22%. This divergence may signal a turning point.
- **QQQQ 10wk rule:** QQQQ has closed above its 10-week average for five straight weeks. Historical context: March–June 2003 = 44 of 47 weeks above 10wk avg; October 2004 and May 2005 = 6-week sprints. A weekly close below 10-week avg is a key weakening signal.
- **MCD breakout trade:** bought after MCD broke to a 5-year high on unusually high volume, bouncing off a 3-month base. Stop: below the breakout day's low. "The stock should not trade below the break-out point."
- **JNJ alert:** 10wk average crossed below 30wk avg, 30wk starting to curve down — ominous pattern, compared to Enron's pattern. A PCF scan flagged JNJ the same way it had flagged Enron and CPN years earlier.
- WPM (Worden market breadth table) shows Dow 30 component stocks weakening: 73% above 30wk avg vs 80% the prior week.
