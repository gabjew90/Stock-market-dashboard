# WW 2022-12-04 — TC2000 gap-up scan: finding stocks after extreme-volume gaps

**Source:** [Day 15 of $QQQ short term up-trend; My TC2000 scan for stocks gapping up on extreme volume; $TMDX and $TMUS](../../raw/posts/2022-12-04-blog-post-day-15-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-time-for-me-to-buy-see-my-tc2000-scan-for-finding-stocks-gapping.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2022/12/blog-post-day-15-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-time-for-me-to-buy-see-my-tc2000-scan-for-finding-stocks-gapping-up-on-extreme-volume-tmdx-and-tmus-are-examples-see-charts/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish introduces a new TC2000 scan for finding stocks that had a large-volume gap-up day in the recent past, then consolidated, and are now resuming their advance. He presents the exact TC2000 formula and explains the rationale: a huge-volume gap-up often indicates institutional buying; the post-gap consolidation creates a BOS-type entry opportunity. TMDX and TMUS are worked examples from December 2022. He also explains his post-decline re-entry philosophy: "people first buy the big caps and big names until they feel more confident."

## Key claims

- **Scan rationale:** stocks beginning major advances often start with a large gap up on unusually high volume (after earnings or a positive announcement). Unable to buy on the gap day, Dr. Wish waits for consolidation and then buys when the stock resumes its advance.
- **TC2000 formula (exact):**
  - Daily condition: `L>H1 and V=maxv20 and H=MaxH10`
    - Low today > yesterday's high (gap up)
    - Volume = the highest volume in the past 20 days
    - Today's high = the highest high in the past 10 days
  - Apply this condition to "true within the last 25 bars" (not just today, but in any of the last 25 trading days)
  - Add it as a column in any watchlist; sort by it; then apply additional BOS/setup filters
- TMDX example: had a recent GLB and was bouncing off the daily 8-EMA after the extreme-volume gap.
- TMUS example: in the midst of a GLB, also bouncing off its 8-EMA.
- Caveat: "a huge gap up can signal the end of a large move rather than the beginning. One must evaluate each stock's chart and trend carefully."
- Re-entry philosophy (post-2022 decline): "the market does climb a wall of worry" — excessive media negativity about inflation/recession can itself be a contrarian signal. Re-enter gradually when key MAs are retaken.
