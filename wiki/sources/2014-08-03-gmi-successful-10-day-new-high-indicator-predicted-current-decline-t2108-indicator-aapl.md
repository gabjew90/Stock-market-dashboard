# WW 2014-08-03 — GMI 10-Day New High Indicator; T2108; AAPL

**Source:** [GMI Successful 10 Day New High Indicator Predicted Current Decline; $T2108 indicator, $AAPL](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2014/08/gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Three topics. First: a detailed retrospective on the GMI Successful 10-Day New High Indicator, showing it had been positive on 97% of days from May 22–July 3, then turned negative on 89% of days from July 7–August 1, presaging a market decline. Second: the fullest explanation of T2108 in this batch — definition (% of NYSE stocks above their 40-day simple MA), source (Worden/TC2000), thresholds (>80% = extended, <10% = near bottom), and how he uses it. Third: the 10-week average rule for individual stocks, with AAPL as a live example — he only holds AAPL as long as it stays above its rising 10-week average.

## Key claims

- GMI Successful 10-Day New High Indicator: counts the % of stocks that hit a 52-week high 10 days ago and have closed higher today than they did 10 days ago. Threshold: ≥50% = positive, adds 1 point to the GMI. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- Failed breakouts in strong stocks are "ominous" — this is the mechanistic story behind the indicator. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- T2108 definition: "the percentage of all NYSE stocks that have closed above their simple moving average of closes the past 40 days." Source: Worden/TC2000, symbol T2108. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- T2108 thresholds: above 80% = extended up; below 10% = near bottom of a steep decline ("time to grit my teeth and buy a market index ETF"). ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- "Markets tend to be extended up when the T2108 is above 80%. When it is below 10%, the market is usually close to the bottom of a steep decline." ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- The 10-week average rule for stocks: "I have found AAPL fine to own as long as it remained above its rising 10 week average." A weekly close below the rising 10-week average is the exit signal. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
- QQQ short-term down-trends since 2006 are often very short; about one quarter last less than 6 days. He often buys a little of the 3X bearish ETF (SQQQ) and adds to it only if the down-trend lasts 5–6 days. ([WW 2014-08-03](../../raw/posts/2014-08-03-gmi-successful-10-day-new-high-indicator-predicted-current-decline-t2108-indicator-aapl.md))
