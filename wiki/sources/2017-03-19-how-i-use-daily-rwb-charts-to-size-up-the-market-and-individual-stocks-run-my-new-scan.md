# WW 2017-03-19 — How I use daily RWB charts to size up the market and individual stocks

**Source:** [How I use daily RWB charts to size up the market and individual stocks; run my new scan](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/03/how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish explains the **daily RWB chart** — a modification of his earlier weekly GMMA chart — as a tool for both market analysis and individual stock entry/exit timing. He introduces the Red Line Count (RLC) metric, shows how QQQ, SPY, and DIA were positioned in March 2017, and describes a TC2000 scan he had published for finding oversold-and-recovering stocks in a daily RWB up-trend. The post explicitly attributes his framework to Stan Weinstein's stage analysis (30-week MA) and Daryl Guppy's GMMA.

## Key claims

- The **weekly** RWB chart shows the longer-term trend but is too slow for timing entries and exits. Dr. Wish shifted his focus to **daily RWB charts** for timing. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- **Red Line Count (RLC):** the count (0–6) of the six shorter-term exponential MAs that the price is currently above. RLC = 6 means price is above all red lines. RLC = 0 means price is below all red lines. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- He added **purple dots** to the daily chart showing each day's low price — useful for visualizing where support has been and where to place stops. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- If the white space between red and blue averages disappears (red and blue converge), he would sell the position. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- At the time, SPY's RLC was 0 — first time since January; DIA's was 2. The Dow Jones Transportation Average had lost its RWB pattern entirely (below all red and blue averages). He flags this as a potential Dow Theory warning. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- Stop placement on individual stocks: either the lowest rising red line (the 15-day EMA, value shown on the chart) or the recent daily low (purple dot), whichever is more conservative. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- His scan (03182017) ran on stocks that hit an all-time high during that year; yielded 59 candidates. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
- Attributes the GMMA framework to Daryl Guppy (guppytraders.com); attributes stage analysis to Stan Weinstein. ([WW 2017-03-19](../../raw/posts/2017-03-19-how-i-use-daily-rwb-charts-to-size-up-the-market-and-individual-stocks-run-my-new-scan.md))
