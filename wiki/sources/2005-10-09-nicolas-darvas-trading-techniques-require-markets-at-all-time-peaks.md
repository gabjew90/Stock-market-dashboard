# WW 2005-10-09 — Nicolas Darvas trading techniques require markets at all-time peaks

**Source:** [Nicolas Darvas trading techniques require markets at all-time peaks](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/10/nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish presents a critical historical analysis of when Darvas's methods actually work. He quotes from Darvas's 1977 book (a post-fortune retrospective) showing Darvas's own stated principles — checking market trend, industry group strength, buying rising stocks — and then contrasts these with Darvas's behavior in his 1960 book, where he did not systematically assess the market in advance. The resolution: Darvas made his fortune during a bull market with the Dow rising 65% to all-time highs (1957–1960). Dr. Wish argues the Darvas/O'Neil approach requires such a tailwind and cautions that it may not produce similar returns until markets return to all-time peaks.

## Key claims

- The period when Darvas made his $2M (18 months ending ~early 1960) coincided with the Dow rising from ~416 (October 1957 bottom) to 688 (January 1960 peak) — a 65% advance and a market trading at all-time highs. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
- Darvas's retrospective (1977) added market-trend and industry-group criteria that were not prominent in his 1960 account — Dr. Wish flags this discrepancy: his stated methods vs his actual behavior differ. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
- Dr. Wish notes that he himself multiplied his IRA **14-fold** during the 1995–2000 Nasdaq bull run, when that index rose 584% — another period of all-time highs. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
- His conclusion (written 2005, with Dow still well below the 2000 peak): growth-stock traders using Darvas/O'Neil techniques "should stay out of U.S. growth stocks until the market indexes reach historic peaks again — maybe in 2016." ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
- Darvas's box system (support/resistance boxes with stops) was how he survived bad markets — repeated stop-outs in failing trades would push him to cash without a formal market-analysis step. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
- William O'Neil and David Ryan are noted as Darvas fans; CANSLIM bears strong resemblance to Darvas's described principles. ([WW 2005-10-09](../../raw/posts/2005-10-09-nicolas-darvas-trading-techniques-require-markets-at-all-time-peaks.md))
