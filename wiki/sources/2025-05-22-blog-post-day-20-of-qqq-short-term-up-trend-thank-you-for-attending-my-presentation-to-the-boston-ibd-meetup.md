# WW 2025-05-22 — Blue dot of happiness: full TC2000 scan formula

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 20 of $QQQ short term up-trend; Thank you for attending my Boston IBD Meetup presentation; See my TC2000 Scan](../../raw/posts/2025-05-22-blog-post-day-20-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-thank-you-for-attending-my-presentation-to-the-boston-ibd-meetup.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2025/05/blog-post-day-20-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-thank-you-for-attending-my-presentation-to-the-boston-ibd-meetup/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Published after a Boston IBD Meetup presentation in May 2025. Dr. Wish reveals the full TC2000 scan formula for his "Blue Dot of Happiness" indicator — the most complete public disclosure of the scan criteria. The blue dot marks the day the 10.1 daily stochastic crosses above 20. The full scan filters for: stock had a blue dot within the last 3 days; stochastic crossed above 20 but was below it the previous day; volume > 20,000 shares (typo: likely 200,000); price > $15; stock in top half of its current day's price bar (Stoc1.1 > 50); within 3 months of a 250-bar monthly high (proxy for near-ATH); optionally touched the lower 15.2 Bollinger Band within last 3 days. He scans a watchlist of stocks previously on IBD50 or MarketSurge. He notes the QQQ exit rule in plain terms: "If QQQ should close back below its 30 week average (501) I will exit all stocks again."

## Key claims

- Blue dot indicator formula: `IIf(Stoc10.1 > 20 AND Stoc10.1[1] < 20, close - 1, null)` — marks the day the 10.1 stochastic crosses above 20; displayed 1 dollar below the day's low on a daily chart.
- Full scan criteria: blue dot in last 3 days; above 20 today, below yesterday; V>20000; C>15; price in top half of bar (Stoc1.1>50); near monthly ATH (250-bar high within last 3 months); optionally near lower 15.2 Bollinger Band.
- He scans against a curated IBD50/MarketSurge watchlist, then applies earnings-date and fundamental checks before buying.
- Exit rule for all stock positions: close back below QQQ's 30-week average triggers a full exit — still active in 2025.
