# WW 2012-06-18 — 1990s trading diary; Darvas scan; GMI buy-signal trigger

**Source:** [An excerpt from my trading diary from the 90's; Market at critical juncture? IBD declares new up-trend; ASPS and QCOR](../../raw/posts/2012-06-18-an-excerpt-from-my-trading-diary-from-the-90s-market-at-critical-juncture.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2012/06/an-excerpt-from-my-trading-diary-from-the-90s-market-at-critical-juncture/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish shares a 1996 trading-diary excerpt showing his philosophy at the time — ignore media commentary, watch price and trend lines, stay skeptical of "buy and hold" advice. In 2012, he explains the GMI buy-signal trigger mechanism explicitly. He also shows ASPS and QCOR from a Darvas-style ATH scan. The post is a useful bridge between his intellectual origins (Darvas, Neill, Livermore) and his 2012 system.

## Key claims

- **GMI buy signal trigger (explicit):** "Two consecutive days with the GMI above 3 would turn the GMI signal to buy." One day above 3 is not enough — he requires the second consecutive day. ([WW 2012-06-18](../../raw/posts/2012-06-18-an-excerpt-from-my-trading-diary-from-the-90s-market-at-critical-juncture.md))
- **Entry confidence rule:** "If an up-trend begins, I will not weigh in heavily until the 5th day. Most new trends that last 5 days can go on considerably longer."
- **Darvas scan:** scans for stocks breaking out to ATHs — used to find ASPS and QCOR independently of IBD lists.
- **Stock selection process:** new-high list → monthly/weekly/daily chart patterns → IBD stock check-up → exact sell price designated at purchase.
- IBD's declaration of a new market up-trend (follow-through day) coincided with a signal near QQQ's short-term trend; Dr. Wish waits for his own indicators, not IBD's timing declaration.
- The T2108 at 39% (neutral); 125 new 52-week highs vs 84 lows — signs of strength. 87% of Nasdaq100 stocks with MACD above signal line.
