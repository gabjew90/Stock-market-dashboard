# WW 2012-07-23 — Stage analysis and green line charts

**Source:** [24th day of QQQ short term up-trend; Stage analysis and green line charts](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2012/07/24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written after an AAII workshop, this post defines two foundational concepts in Dr. Wish's methodology. First: stage analysis (from Stan Weinstein) — he only buys long when both the market AND the individual stock are in a Stage 2 up-trend, meaning they are following along their rising 30-week average. Second: green line charts — on a monthly price chart, draw a horizontal green line wherever a stock first reaches an all-time high and then stays below that level for 3 or more months. When the stock later breaks above the green line, that is the signal to buy. He uses MSFT's 1990–2000 run as a historical example, and EW as a current candidate.

## Key claims

- "I only buy long when the market is in a Stage 2 up-trend and if the stock or ETF I am buying is also in a Stage 2 up-trend." ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- Stage 2 is characterized by a stock following along its rising 30-week average. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- When the QQQ or SPY closes below the 30-week average, he becomes "very defensive": no new buys, raise stops; if the average turns down, typically exits the market. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- The green line: a horizontal line drawn on a monthly chart at the all-time high level once a stock has held below that high for 3 or more months. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- A break above the green line — appearing on the new-high list — is the GLB entry signal. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- One component of the GMI counts whether the QQQ has closed above its 30-week average. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
- The GMI was at 5 (of 6) and GMI2 at 4 at the time of writing. ([WW 2012-07-23](../../raw/posts/2012-07-23-24th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-stage-analysis-and-green-line-charts.md))
