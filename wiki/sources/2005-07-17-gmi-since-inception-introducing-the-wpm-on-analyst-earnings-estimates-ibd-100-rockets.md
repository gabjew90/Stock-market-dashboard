# WW 2005-07-17 — GMI since inception; introducing the WPM

**Source:** [GMI since inception; introducing the WPM; on analyst earnings estimates; IBD 100 rockets](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/07/gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish shows a chart of GMI readings over the prior weeks (illustrating how quickly it can move from +3 to +6 in a single day) and introduces the WishingWealth Pulse of the Market (WPM). The WPM is a composite table tracking the Dow 30, S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, S&P 400 (mid-cap) and S&P 600 (small-cap) indexes: for each, whether the index closed above its 30-day and 30-week averages, and what percentage of component stocks closed above those averages. He attributes the usefulness of the 30-week average to Stan Weinstein and credits it with keeping him out of the 2000 bear and getting him back in for the 2003 rally.

## Key claims

- The GMI can go from +3 to +6 in a single day; "It took only one day for the GMI to go from +3 to +6. This is an example of how bad it is to marry a scenario." ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))
- The WPM uses 30-day and 30-week averages as trend indicators; Dr. Wish calls these "the best trend indicators for market movement" based on 40+ years of trading. ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))
- The 30-week average's reversal in 2000 signaled exit; its reversal in 2003 signaled re-entry — he credits Weinstein for alerting him to this. ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))
- He calls the 30-day average "the most reliable indicator of the short term trend." ([WW 2005-07-17](../../raw/posts/2005-07-17-gmi-since-inception-introducing-the-wpm-on-analyst-earnings-estimates-ibd-100-rockets.md))
