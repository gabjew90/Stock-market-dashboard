# WW 2026-05-10 — 5-day EMA post-GLB exit rule; 4wk>10wk>30wk up-trend

## What it covers

A May 2026 post confirming the 4wk>10wk>30wk weekly alignment as the hallmark of a powerful Stage 2 up-trend, with QQQ's 10-week average finally crossing back above its 30-week average. Dr. Wish introduces (or reinforces) the **5-day EMA post-GLB trailing exit rule** — hold a GLB stock while it continues to trend above its 5-day exponential moving average; place a sell stop below the low of the day it closes beneath the 5-day EMA. INTC and QQQ are shown as live examples trending along their 5-day EMA after GLBs. The post notes that because QQQ closed the week well above its 4-week average, a pullback to kiss the 4-week average (~675) would be the next preferred buy point.

## Key claims

- When QQQ's 4wk > 10wk > 30wk average alignment holds, it is a "powerful up-trend to ride" (2026).
- Post-GLB, one way to manage a position is to track the 5-day EMA: hold while the stock closes above it; sell stop below the low of any day it closes beneath it.
- The same 5-day EMA trailing rule applies to index ETFs (QQQ shown) as well as individual stocks (INTC shown).
- When the index closes the week far above its 4-week average, expect a pullback to kiss the 4-week average before the next leg up — that retest is a buy point.
- GLBs in the May 2026 rally were "rocketing higher" — the rule is still in force and working.
