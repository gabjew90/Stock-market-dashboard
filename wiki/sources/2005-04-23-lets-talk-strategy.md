# WW 2005-04-23 — Let's Talk Strategy

**Source:** [Let's Talk Strategy](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/04/lets-talk-strategy/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

The first post where Dr. Wish explains his trading philosophy at length. He traces his intellectual lineage through five books — Darvas, O'Neil, Livermore (via Lefevre), Weinstein, and Neill — and distills the core lessons each contributed. He frames his approach as a synthesis: buy stocks that have already shown strength (technical), focus on the right market environment (the "M" in CANSLIM), cut losses quickly, average up not down, and use stage analysis (Weinstein's 30-week moving average) to time bull and bear cycles.

## Key claims

- More than 70% of all stocks move in the same direction as the major market indexes. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
- He learned from Darvas to buy stocks at all-time highs with great products; from O'Neil, that a successful trader can have many small losses as long as gains are large. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
- He never goes long when his general market trend indicators suggest a declining trend — he sells all holdings and sometimes buys puts. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
- Weinstein's 30-week moving average was the tool that alerted him to exit in 2000 and re-enter in 2003. He references this in passing here; the 2005-07-17 post elaborates. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
- From Livermore: make a pilot buy; average up, never down; sit tight with a winner; concentrate on few positions rather than diversifying. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
- He trades in a tax-sheltered IRA to avoid wash-sale rules and freely re-enter positions. ([WW 2005-04-23](../../raw/posts/2005-04-23-lets-talk-strategy.md))
