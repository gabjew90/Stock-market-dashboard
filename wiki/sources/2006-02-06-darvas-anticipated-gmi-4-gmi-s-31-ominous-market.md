# WW 2006-02-06 — Darvas/Wyckoff noise isolation; GMI-S short-term gauge

**Source:** [Darvas anticipated; GMI: +4; GMI-S: 31; Ominous market](../../raw/posts/2006-02-06-darvas-anticipated-gmi-4-gmi-s-31-ominous-market.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2006/02/darvas-anticipated-gmi-4-gmi-s-31-ominous-market/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

The post opens with a verbatim quote from Richard Wyckoff (1924): that the best trading of his career occurred when he isolated himself from Wall Street news and judged solely from price action — predating Darvas's identical approach by thirty years. Dr. Wish draws the parallel to the modern internet/CNBC era, arguing that media access has made noise avoidance harder, but the lesson remains: concentrate on price and your own analysis. The market update introduces the GMI-S ("short-term" sub-index) with its range of 0–100 and its sharp drop from 75 to 31 in one week as an early warning indicator.

## Key claims

- Wyckoff (1924), verbatim: trading from end-of-day prices cabled in from afar, ignoring all news and gossip, produced his best results. "All he needs is the highest, lowest and last prices." ([WW 2006-02-06](../../raw/posts/2006-02-06-darvas-anticipated-gmi-4-gmi-s-31-ominous-market.md))
- Darvas did the same thing thirty years later, physically leaving the country and reading only stock prices in Barron's — validating Wyckoff's method.
- Dr. Wish's lesson: the more he insulates himself from outside influences and focuses on price movement, the better he trades. "One must be a loner and confident in one's own judgments."
- GMI-S is a short-term sub-index (0–100 scale) measuring breadth within the index components. A rapid fall from 75 to 31 in a week is a warning of short-term deterioration even when the GMI itself (at +4) remains positive.
- The QQQQ had just crossed below its 10-week average — day D-1 of a new short-term down-trend.
