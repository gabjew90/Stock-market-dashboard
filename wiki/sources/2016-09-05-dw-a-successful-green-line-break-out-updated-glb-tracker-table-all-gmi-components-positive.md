# WW 2016-09-05 — DW GLB worked example; GLB tracker table; weekly volume scan

**Source:** [$DW: A Successful Green Line Break-out; updated GLB tracker table; All GMI components positive](../../raw/posts/2016-09-05-dw-a-successful-green-line-break-out-updated-glb-tracker-table-all-gmi-components-positive.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2016/09/dw-a-successful-green-line-break-out-updated-glb-tracker-table-all-gmi-components-positive/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A September 2016 post written for a class of 160 freshmen. DW (Drew Industries) is shown as a worked example of a GLB rocket stock: it broke above its green line on the highest weekly volume in months, showed rising relative strength versus QQQ even during several QQQ declines (amber vertical lines show QQQ correction endpoints), and went on to advance strongly. The post also describes the weekly scan he runs each weekend to find GLBs: stocks that closed at a new 52-week high on Friday, with total weekly volume at least 10% above its recent average, and trading at least 500,000 shares total — then verifies the stock also hit an all-time high. The GLB tracker table (sidebar) is described as a sentiment and education tool, not a buy list. Nine of that week's 27 scan results also closed above their last green line top (MELI, SHOP, CMN, MOMO, FOXF, INFO, ACGL, UNF, AOSL).

## Key claims

- Weekly GLB scan: new 52-week high Friday close + weekly volume 10%+ above recent average + 500,000+ shares total = GLB candidate list.
- Relative strength versus QQQ during corrections is a key confirming factor for a GLB candidate (shown by the amber vertical lines on the DW chart).
- GLB tracker table: a list of recent GLBs updated each Friday; tracks how stocks perform after their breakout date. Its purpose is sentiment/education — it is a biased sample (only stocks that subsequently continued to ATH are listed).
- "I look for a strong stock that has traded at its all-time peak price and that has now rested, or consolidated, usually for less than a year."
