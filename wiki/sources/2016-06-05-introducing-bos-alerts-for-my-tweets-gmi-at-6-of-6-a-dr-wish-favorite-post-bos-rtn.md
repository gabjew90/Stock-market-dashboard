# WW 2016-06-05 — Introducing BOS (Bounce off Support)

**Source:** [Introducing BOS alerts for my tweets; GMI at 6; BOS: $RTN](../../raw/posts/2016-06-05-introducing-bos-alerts-for-my-tweets-gmi-at-6-of-6-a-dr-wish-favorite-post-bos-rtn.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2016/06/introducing-bos-alerts-for-my-tweets-gmi-at-6-of-6-a-dr-wish-favorite-post-bos-rtn/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

This post formally introduces the **BOS (Bounce off Support)** strategy, which Dr. Wish describes as his preferred alternative to buying GLBs at the moment of breakout. The strategy buys a strong "rocket stock" that has become oversold — pulling back to or below support — and is bouncing back up. He sets a TC2000 alert for BOS candidates and places a stop just below the bounce level immediately on purchase. The post reflects a key evolution: he explicitly states he now likes this approach *better* than trading GLBs at the breakout moment.

## Key claims

- The BOS set-up is for a rising market (GMI on Buy signal or QQQ short-term up-trend). Only trade BOS when the market context is favorable.
- Buy a strong rocket stock that is oversold and bouncing off support; place a sell stop immediately *below* the bounce or support level. This keeps losses small because the stop is close to entry.
- The primary oversold/support indicator is not specified in this post (he says "I will not specify here") — but he mentions TC2000 alerts and the fact that he is looking for a stock near support (e.g., bouncing off a Bollinger Band or moving average).
- "I know that a good percentage of these entries will fail, but the name of the game is to lose very little when it fails, to exit quickly, and to retain stocks that behave."
- The key insight, stated after 50 years of trading: "I take an unemotional and detached attitude, making my purchase, setting an immediate GTC stop loss order, and then letting the market decide whether I will profit or lose."
- RTN is the concrete example: Dr. Wish bought RTN and placed a stop below 129; RTN held the bounce and moved higher.
- He tweets BOS alerts intraday (@WishingWealth) — the tweet includes the stock symbol and the price at which he considers the trade a failure (where he would place the stop).
