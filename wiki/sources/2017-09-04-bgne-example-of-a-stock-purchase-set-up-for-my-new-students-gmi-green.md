# WW 2017-09-04 — BGNE: monthly/weekly/daily chain for a trade setup

**Source:** [$BGNE: Example of a stock purchase set-up for my new students; GMI: GREEN](../../raw/posts/2017-09-04-bgne-example-of-a-stock-purchase-set-up-for-my-new-students-gmi-green.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/09/bgne-example-of-a-stock-purchase-set-up-for-my-new-students-gmi-green/) · tier: trade_example

## What it covers

A step-by-step worked example of Dr. Wish's full trade setup process, using BGNE (BeiGene, a 2016 IPO biotech). It demonstrates the three-chart chain — monthly (GLB check), weekly (yellowband/Stage 2 confirmation), and daily (BOS entry). He explicitly shows how to define the stop *before* entering, and explains the difference between the daily stop (30-day or 50-day MA level) and the longer-term stop (below 10-week average on the weekly chart, used for holding a position through normal volatility). He emphasizes checking next earnings date as a prerequisite.

## Key claims

- Three-chart analysis chain: (1) monthly chart → confirm stock is above its green line top; (2) weekly chart → confirm yellowband up-trend (10wk > 30wk with stock above the 10-week); (3) daily chart → find a BOS entry (bounce off the 30-day or 50-day moving average).
- Checking earnings date is explicitly mentioned: BGNE's next earnings were expected 11/9/17, so there was no imminent earnings release risk.
- Entry timing: BGNE bounced off its 30-day average (red line) on a Friday. Stop is set just below the low of the bounce off the 30-day average ($69.29) or at the 30-day average itself ($71.15) for a smaller loss.
- "The key is to sell out quickly if a stock does not act as predicted when one purchased it."
- Holding rule distinction: if the stock advances, switch to the weekly chart as the reference. "Big money is possible in holding a yellowband stock as long as the pattern holds." Checking daily gyrations often leads to being "shaken out of a solid yellowband stock." He prefers to check the *weekly* chart before selling.
- BGNE came public in 2016 — recent IPO + initial GLB + strong partner announcement (Celgene deal) = high-quality candidate.
- GMI at 6/6 = full Green signal.
