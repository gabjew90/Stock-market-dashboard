# WW 2020-09-27 — Monitoring GLBs during corrections; weekly chart rule for holding winners

**Source:** [This week will determine if tech down-trend will continue. Monitoring GLBs to find leaders during a correction — $PINS, $PTON, $CRWD; Follow the weekly chart $ZM, $FVRR, $TSLA](../../raw/posts/2020-09-27-this-week-will-determine-if-tech-down-trend-will-continue-monitoring-glbs-to-find-leaders-during-a-correction.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2020/09/this-week-will-determine-if-tech-down-trend-will-continue-monitoring-glbs-to-find-leaders-during-a-correction/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A September 2020 post during a tech correction (GMI=1, Red). Dr. Wish's approach to corrections: watch the daily and weekly new-high lists for stocks with above-average volume GLBs — these are the market leaders being born during the correction. PINS (a recent IPO) had a beautiful GLB and was on his watch list. He walks through PTON, ZM, FVRR, TSLA, and CRWD on weekly charts, illustrating the "hold above 10-week average" rule for managing post-GLB positions. Key insight: "I find that focusing on daily charts often causes me to exit a winner too early. A rule I have posted on my screen: BEFORE SELLING, LOOK AT WEEKLY CHART." He also notes that an initial GLB retest (CRWD pulled back to retest its GLB level) is a valid secondary entry.

## Key claims

- Market leaders are born during corrections: watch for GLBs on the new-high list even while GMI is Red.
- After a GLB, manage the position on a weekly chart using the 10-week average (blue dotted line) as the hold signal.
- "BEFORE SELLING, LOOK AT WEEKLY CHART" — a rule posted on his screen, still active in 2020.
- T2108 at a low of 13% during this correction, recovered to 23% — not at the below-10% extreme buy signal.
- A post-GLB retest (stock pulls back to the green line level) is a secondary entry opportunity.
