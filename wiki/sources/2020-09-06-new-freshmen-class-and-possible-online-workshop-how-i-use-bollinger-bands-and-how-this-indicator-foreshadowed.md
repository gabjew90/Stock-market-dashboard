# WW 2020-09-06 — Bollinger Bands: entry and extension signals

**Source:** [New freshmen class; How I use Bollinger Bands and how this indicator foreshadowed this decline](../../raw/posts/2020-09-06-new-freshmen-class-and-possible-online-workshop-how-i-use-bollinger-bands-and-how-this-indicator-foreshadowed.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2020/09/new-freshmen-class-and-possible-online-workshop-how-i-use-bollinger-bands-and-how-this-indicator-foreshadowed-this-decline-just-a-brief-shake-out-short-and-long-term-trends-of-the-market-remain-up/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A formal explanation of how Dr. Wish uses Bollinger Bands (BB) in his trading. He uses a **15-day moving average with 2-standard-deviation bands** (the same 15.2 Bollinger Band referenced in the BOS posts). He explains two signal types: (1) trading above the upper band = extension/potential reversal or breakout confirmation; (2) trading near the lower band = potential bounce/oversold. QQQ, AAPL, and ZM are used as concrete examples from the September 2020 market decline. He also introduces his "green dot" signal (TC2000-based short-term up-trend resumption indicator).

## Key claims

- Bollinger Bands create a "95% confidence interval" around the 15-day average: nearly all prices will stay within the two bands.
- Two signal types: (1) a stock rising above the upper BB in a consolidation → breakout signal; (2) a stock that has been rising reaches or exceeds the upper BB → can signal end of the short-term move; (3) lower BB = bounce opportunity or continuation of a larger decline.
- QQQ example (Sept 2020): declined to near the lower BB and bounced — "a failure of one of these bounces would suggest to me the likelihood of their further decline."
- AAPL example: at its recent top, AAPL traded outside its upper BB and reversed to close below it — "a sign that it was extended and could decline." The black dot (TC2000 oversold signal) and a green dot (short-term up-trend start) are shown on the chart.
- ZM example: ZM broke above its upper BB on large volume — a sign of extreme extension. The following day produced a large decline.
- Market-breadth cross-check: when >50% of Nasdaq 100 stocks traded above their upper BB on Wednesday (a day Dr. Wish mentions in this post), it foreshadowed the large decline. On Friday only 2% traded above and 42% were below their lower BB — suggesting a bounce.
- His preference: "I prefer the BB because I think it works better for me." He notes that many traders use the 21-day EMA instead of the 15-day MA in the BB, and observes "if multiple indicators work, they must be somewhat correlated."
