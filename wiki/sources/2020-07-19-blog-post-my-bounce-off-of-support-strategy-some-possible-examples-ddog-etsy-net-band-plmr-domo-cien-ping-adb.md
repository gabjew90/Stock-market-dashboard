# WW 2020-07-19 — Bounce-off-support strategy: green dot stochastic + stop below recent low

**Source:** [Blog post: My bounce off of support strategy; Some possible examples: $DDOG, $ETSY, $NET, $BAND, $PLMR, $DOMO, $CIEN, $PING, $ADBE, $AYX, $DSGX](../../raw/posts/2020-07-19-blog-post-my-bounce-off-of-support-strategy-some-possible-examples-ddog-etsy-net-band-plmr-domo-cien-ping-adb.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2020/07/blog-post-my-bounce-off-of-support-strategy-some-possible-examples-ddog-etsy-net-band-plmr-domo-cien-ping-adb/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A July 2020 post describing a complementary entry strategy to GLBs: buy strong growth stocks that have become oversold and are bouncing up off support. The green dot signal (low fast stochastic crosses above slow stochastic) defines oversold. The mechanics: once the oversold bounce occurs, place a stop just below the recent low (the bounce day's low). This gives a mechanically defined entry and a tight, low-risk exit. Dr. Wish contrasts this with the GLB approach: in a GLB the stop is at the green line and can be far from the purchase price if the stock moved quickly; the bounce-off-support approach narrows this gap. SEDG is the detailed example. The strategy targets stocks that have already appreciated significantly — the same Livermore/Darvas/O'Neil/Minervini principle of buying strong stocks, not weak ones.

## Key claims

- The bounce-off-support entry (green dot + stop below bounce low) minimizes initial loss compared to a GLB entry where the stop is the green line (potentially far away).
- Green dot signal: "low fast stochastic turns up and crosses above the slow stochastic" — the TC2000 indicator for this bounced oversold condition.
- "The shake out often sets up a nice subsequent advance" — high-volume down days before a bounce can be bullish because weak holders sold and shorts are set up.
- Do not jump the gun — wait for the bounce to occur; the stock could continue declining.
- Stop-loss rule: place GTC stop below the recent low (the bounce low), so that you are automatically exited if the setup fails.
