# WW 2016-10-02 — O'Neil 1995 workshop diary; LMAT BOS setup; two-close GLB rule

**Source:** [My trading diary entry from William O'Neil's workshop in 1995; a set-up for buying $LMAT; $HEIA – Cup and handle break-out](../../raw/posts/2016-10-02-my-trading-diary-entry-from-william-oneils-workshop-in-1995-a-set-up-for-buying-lmat-heia-cup-and-handle-brea.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 2 October 2016. The post has two distinct sections. First, Dr. Wish quotes verbatim from his November 1995 personal trading diary, describing what he observed at a William O'Neil lecture in person. Second, he walks through a complete LMAT trade setup combining GLB, RWB, and BOS on the Bollinger Band — used as a teaching example for his undergraduate students.

## Key claims

**The 1995 O'Neil diary excerpt (Copyright, Dr. Eric Wish trading diary, 11/11/1995):**
- O'Neil was "advising institutional investors to buy the current fad internet stocks like C-Cube and Netscape" — contrary to his own written rules requiring high EPS.
- O'Neil said "volume and price action was more important than demonstrated earnings growth." A "dramatic rise in volume could be the most important factor."
- O'Neil's major criterion at that lecture: "stocks growing 100% in price a year or with 99 Relative Strength."
- Dr. Wish noted: "I got the distinct impression that he was saying that volume and price action was more important than demonstrated earnings growth." He wanted to ask O'Neil whether he consciously breaks his own rule requiring high EPS.
- Dr. Wish has written "200+ pages" of trading diary from the 1990s bull market.

**LMAT setup (October 2016):**
- LMAT hit an all-time high on 8/30. Dr. Wish drew a green line at the prior peak (held ≥3 months on the monthly chart) — standard GLB detection.
- Weekly Guppy/RWB chart confirmed: all 6 short-term (red) EMA lines rising above the longer-term (blue) lines. IBD composite ranking: 99.
- **Two-close GLB rule in action:** LMAT closed above the green line twice initially — but then traded back below for 3 days. "I immediately sell failed break-outs." Then LMAT had a second, larger break-out on considerably higher volume.
- **BOS post-GLB entry:** After the failed break-out (or if you missed the second break-out), the next opportunity was a bounce off the rising 30-day average *and* the lower daily 15.2 Bollinger Band on increased volume. "This is one of my favorite set-ups."
- **Stop placement:** "Place a sell stop around the low of the bounce, near $19.29." Rule: "Must sell immediately if the bounce bought on does not hold."
- Scan: he uses TC2000 to scan specifically for this combination (GLB stock + bounce off 30-day average + BB 15.2 signal).
