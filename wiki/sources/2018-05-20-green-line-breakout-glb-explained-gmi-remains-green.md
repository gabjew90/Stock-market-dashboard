# WW 2018-05-20 — Green line breakout (GLB) explained

**Source:** [Green line breakout (GLB) explained; GMI remains Green](../../raw/posts/2018-05-20-green-line-breakout-glb-explained-gmi-remains-green.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2018/05/green-line-breakout-glb-explained-gmi-remains-green/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A comprehensive 2018 explanation of the GLB concept, aimed at a public audience. Covers the base-and-breakout logic, the "already doubled" attribute, the overhead-supply argument for buying at ATHs, the strict sell rule ("immediately if it comes back below its green line"), re-entry after a failed GLB, pyramiding a successful position until a close below the 30-week average, and the GLB Tracker list Dr. Wish maintained on the blog sidebar.

## Key claims

- The GLB requires "at least a 3 month base" at an all-time high; preference for increased volume on the breakout.
- A stock that "has recently doubled is likely to double again" — the Darvas principle applied to the pre-GLB run.
- **Strict sell rule:** sell the stock "immediately if it comes back below its green line. No hesitation or remorse." Buying back after a subsequent successful GLB is explicitly allowed and encouraged.
- After a GLB, one can re-enter at the 4-week or 10-week moving average during the subsequent advance as long as reasonable sell stops are set.
- **Pyramiding rule (2018):** "if one is fortunate enough to hop on a successful GLB stock and pyramid one's position, it might be a good strategy to stay aboard until it closes below its rising 30 week average." — a longer hold rule than the mental-stop-at-green-line that applies to the initial entry.
- GLB Tracker: a sidebar list of recent GLBs maintained as a sentiment indicator ("how likely recent initially successful GLBs are continuing to rise"), not as a buy list.
