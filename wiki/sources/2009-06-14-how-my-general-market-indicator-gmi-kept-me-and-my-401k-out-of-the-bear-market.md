# WW 2009-06-14 — How my GMI kept me and my 401K out of the bear market

**Source:** [How my General Market Indicator (GMI) and technical analysis kept me and my 401K out of the bear market](../../raw/posts/2009-06-14-how-my-general-market-indicator-gmi-kept-me-and-my-401k-out-of-the-bear-market.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2009/06/how-my-general-market-indicator-gmi-kept-me-and-my-401k-out-of-the-bear-market/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written in June 2009 after the March 2009 bear-market bottom, this post provides a retrospective weekly GMI chart covering the entire 2008–2009 bear market. It shows how the GMI declined to 0 in September 2008 and remained there through December, then hit 4 in early April 2009 and has remained 4 since. He explains his re-entry in June 2009: 15% of pension moved from money market to a growth mutual fund, pilot buys in SYNA and HMSY. He also states the condition for a tradeable advance: 10-week average must be above the 30-week average.

## Key claims

- The GMI provides a visual track record across the 2008–2009 bear: last reading of GMI=6 was in a May 2008 bounce; declined to 0 by September 2008; brief hit of 4 in a bounce; quickly returned to 0; remained at 0 through December 2008; hit 4 in early April 2009 and stayed at 4 since.
- He goes long when GMI hits 4, not waiting for GMI=6. The GMI "could have differed during the week from the end-of-week reading" — he acknowledges the chart shows end-of-week snapshots only.
- Condition for a tradeable advance: "the 10 week average (dotted line) is now above the 30 week average (red line). This is a key characteristic of tradable advances."
- He has moved 15% of pension out of the money market into a growth mutual fund and averaged up in SYNA and HMSY. Re-entry is cautious: "pilot buy, only average up if the stock behaves as expected."
- Key aphorism (O'Neil): "All stocks are bad unless they go up." Every loss brings him to the next winner.
- The truck-in-the-road metaphor: when the market is trending you don't argue with it — you act. He does not wait for confirming news.
