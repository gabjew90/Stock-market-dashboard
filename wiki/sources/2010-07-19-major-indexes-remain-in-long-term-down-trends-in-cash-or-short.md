# WW 2010-07-19 — 14x IRA; stochastic 10,4,4 timing; QQQQ 10wk/30wk rule

**Source:** [Major indexes remain in longer term down-trends; in cash or short](../../raw/posts/2010-07-19-major-indexes-remain-in-long-term-down-trends-in-cash-or-short.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2010/07/major-indexes-remain-in-long-term-down-trends-in-cash-or-short/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A self-introduction post that updates the track record (14× IRA since 1995 as of July 2010) and explains how Dr. Wish kept his university pension safely in cash through 2000-2002 and 2008. Introduces the **10,4,4 stochastic** as a short-term timing tool for reading overbought/oversold levels within a trend. Articulates the weekly down-trend rule clearly: when the QQQ/QQQQ's 10-week average crosses below its 30-week average, he is in cash or short on the long side. The post is written during the summer 2010 correction.

## Key claims

- Track record explicitly updated to **14×** (fourteen-fold) IRA since 1995 as of the post date. Earlier posts said 13× (2009); this is the next documented increment. ([WW 2010-07-19](../../raw/posts/2010-07-19-major-indexes-remain-in-long-term-down-trends-in-cash-or-short.md))
- University pension kept safely in cash through "all of the major market declines since 1998" and reinvested in subsequent advances.
- Stochastic 10,4,4: when it reaches **very overbought levels** on the daily chart, a market decline is likely; prior declines all ended with the stochastic "well below 50." He uses this to time buys and sells as a secondary overlay (not as a primary signal).
- QQQ/QQQQ 10-week average crossing below the 30-week average on the weekly chart = confirmed weekly down-trend. When this has occurred, he will not trade long. "I cannot make money trading on the long side when these indexes are below their 10 week averages."
- Counter-trend short-term up-trends occur within a weekly down-trend; he distinguishes daily signals from weekly regime.
- GMI at 2 (of 6), GMI-R at 4 (of 10) during this post; T2108 at 46% (neutral).
