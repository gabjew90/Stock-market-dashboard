# WW 2013-11-24 — GMI 3X ETF strategy beats IBD 50 stocks

**Source:** [GMI based strategy using 3X ETF's beats IBD 50 stocks](../../raw/posts/2013-11-24-gmi-based-strategy-using-3x-etfs-beats-ibd-50-stocks.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2013/11/gmi-based-strategy-using-3x-etfs-beats-ibd-50-stocks/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish compares holding TQQQ or UPRO (3X leveraged ETFs) from a GMI buy signal versus picking individual stocks from IBD 50 lists. The analysis covers the period 2013-09-04 to 2013-11-22 across seven IBD 50 lists, three major indexes, and several ETFs. The conclusion surprises him: simply holding TQQQ from the GMI buy signal beat approximately 90% of IBD 50 stocks over this period, and beat stock picking at the 30%-gain level entirely.

## Key claims

- TQQQ gained **+30%** and UPRO gained **+30%** from the GMI buy signal on 9/4 to 11/22. QLD (2X) gained +20%. SPY gained +9.1%, QQQ +9.5%.
- IBD 50 stocks outperform the broader indexes at the +20% and +30% level (8%–14% achieved +30%) vs 0%–3% for major index stocks. But TQQQ/UPRO beat essentially all of them.
- Dr. Wish's lesson, relearned repeatedly: "when the GMI issues a buy signal, I should begin accumulating QLD or TQQQ instead of trying to find the rare stock that will eventually beat them."
- The advantage of leveraged ETFs: easier to focus on one index trend signal (exit when GMI goes RED) vs managing 10+ individual stock positions.
- IBD 50 stocks show no advantage in the probability of gaining *at all* (76%–82%) vs S&P 500 (87%) or Dow (90%). Their edge is in the magnitude of gains, not the hit rate.
