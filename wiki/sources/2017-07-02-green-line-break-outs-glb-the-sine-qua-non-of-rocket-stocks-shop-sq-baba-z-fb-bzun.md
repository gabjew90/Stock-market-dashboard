# WW 2017-07-02 — GLB: the sine qua non of rocket stocks

**Source:** [Green Line Break-outs (GLB), the sine qua non of rocket stocks; $SHOP, $SQ, $BABA, $Z, $FB, $BZUN](../../raw/posts/2017-07-02-green-line-break-outs-glb-the-sine-qua-non-of-rocket-stocks-shop-sq-baba-z-fb-bzun.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/07/green-line-break-outs-glb-the-sine-qua-non-of-rocket-stocks-shop-sq-baba-z-fb-bzun/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A methodical explanation of the intellectual history and precise mechanics of the GLB, drawing a direct line from Darvas and Livermore through Dr. Wish's monthly-chart refinement. It includes Darvas's explicit "no exceptions" rule (always at all-time highs), the "already doubled" attribute, and the observation that Darvas's daily-price box approach led to whipsaws that prompted Dr. Wish to switch to monthly charts. It provides six worked GLB examples (SHOP, SQ, BABA, Z, FB, BZUN) and explains the re-entry rule after a failed GLB.

## Key claims

- Darvas's key rule: to answer whether a stock needed to always be at an all-time high — "no exceptions to this criterion." Dr. Wish adopted this.
- Darvas also required a stock to have **already doubled in the past year** — a predictor of further gains.
- Daily-price box approach produced whipsaws; switching to **monthly charts** (3+ months below the prior ATH) solved this.
- GLB definition: on a monthly chart, draw the green line at the historic peak after the stock has failed to surpass it for **at least 3 months (3 bars on a monthly bar chart)**; alert set in TC2000; buy when it exceeds the green line and closes above it on above-average volume.
- Exit rule: "If it closes back below the green line at anytime afterwards, I exit." Re-entry is explicit: "If it retakes the green line I often buy it back." (No wash-sale issue in a tax-deferred IRA.)
- IPO GLB: Jesse Livermore specifically said a recent IPO that forms a base and breaks to an all-time high is a "screaming buy."
- Examples include FB having multiple GLBs and each one proving profitable.
