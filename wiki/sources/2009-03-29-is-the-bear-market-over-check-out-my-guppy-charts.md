# WW 2009-03-29 — Bear market over? Guppy charts and trend hierarchy

**Source:** [Is the bear market over? Check out my "Guppy" charts](../../raw/posts/2009-03-29-is-the-bear-market-over-check-out-my-guppy-charts.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 29 March 2009, near the early stages of the recovery from the March 2009 lows. Dr. Wish explains how he uses Guppy (GMMA) **weekly** charts to evaluate whether a true bull-market bottom has been established — distinct from a short-term bounce within a down-trend. He introduces the explicit three-level trend hierarchy he operates within, and explains why the pension stays in cash even though the trading IRA can take small long positions in a daily up-trend that is occurring within a longer-term weekly/monthly down-trend.

## Key claims

- "Only liars and lucky people can really call a bear market bottom close to its occurrence. Pundits on TV usually felt comfortable calling a new bull market about six months after the actual bottom."
- **Trend hierarchy stated explicitly:** each trader must define their trading time interval. Dr. Wish's primary frame is the weekly trend. He stays mainly in cash in the IRA during a weekly down-trend even if a daily up-trend is underway.
- The Guppy/GMMA weekly chart bottom signal: short-term moving averages (black) must be **above** the long-term averages (red) at a sustained bull bottom. In March 2009 the chart shows these have not yet crossed — he remains cautious.
- Comparison to the 1974 bear market bottom: similar depth decline; the Guppy weekly chart showed the same pattern of short-term averages rising above long-term averages at the eventual bottom.
- T2108 at 70% (near "overbought territory where prior rallies have topped out") — QQQQ is above its 10-week average; 93% of Nasdaq 100 stocks with daily MACD above signal — signs of short-term strength but not yet a long-term trend reversal.
- "Contrary to the pundits, there is plenty of time to commit university pension money back into the long side once a solid new up-trend has begun."
