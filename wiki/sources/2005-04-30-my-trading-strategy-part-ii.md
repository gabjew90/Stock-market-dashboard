# WW 2005-04-30 — My Trading Strategy, Part II

**Source:** [My Trading Strategy, Part II](../../raw/posts/2005-04-30-my-trading-strategy-part-ii.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/04/my-trading-strategy-part-ii/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

The second strategy post. Dr. Wish uses a rocket-launch metaphor to argue for technical analysis over fundamental analysis: a rocket (stock) that has already lifted off and is climbing is a better bet than one sitting on the launch pad with good specs. He explicitly favors a blend — the rocket must show real thrust (volume, price action) AND have the right capabilities (some fundamental quality). He ties market environment back to the theme: even a good rocket gets thrown off course by bad weather (adverse market conditions). This is an early articulation of why he always checks the general market trend before buying any individual stock.

## Key claims

- A stock at a new all-time high with increasing volume ("extraordinary thrust") is preferable to one that has not yet launched, regardless of fundamental promise. ([WW 2005-04-30](../../raw/posts/2005-04-30-my-trading-strategy-part-ii.md))
- Darvas's blend: buy launched rockets (technical), but know something about the rocket's capabilities (fundamentals). ([WW 2005-04-30](../../raw/posts/2005-04-30-my-trading-strategy-part-ii.md))
- "Just as bad weather can delay a launch or throw a rocket off course, so can the overall market environment." The best stock-picking rules fail in an adverse market. ([WW 2005-04-30](../../raw/posts/2005-04-30-my-trading-strategy-part-ii.md))
- An exception he likes: a stock that reached new heights, fell back for a while, then broke through to a new high — the base-and-breakout pattern. ([WW 2005-04-30](../../raw/posts/2005-04-30-my-trading-strategy-part-ii.md))
