# WW 2011-01-04 — 2010 ETF year-in-review; TQQQ beats individual stocks

**Source:** [2010 ETF Performance: Why search for individual stocks when one can just ride the leveraged ETF's?](../../raw/posts/2011-01-04-2010-etf-performance-why-search-for-individual-stocks-when-one-can-just-ride-the-leveraged-etfs.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 4 January 2011 on Day 30 of a QQQ short-term up-trend (preceded by a 51-day up-trend, with 81 of 83 prior days in an up-trend). Dr. Wish publishes a performance table of major indexes, ETFs, and leveraged ETFs for 2010, reaching the same conclusion he has stated before: if you can identify the market trend, riding a leveraged index ETF is easier and nearly always more profitable than searching for individual rocket stocks.

## Key claims

- **2010 performance data:** SPY +13.4%, DIA +11.1%, QQQQ +19.0%, QLD (2× QQQQ) +36.9%, **TQQQ (3× QQQQ) +66.8%**, DGP (2× gold) +62.4%, **AGQ (2× silver) +182.4%**, GLD +29.3%.
- Only the top 10% of Nasdaq 100 individual stocks advanced 60.4% or more — nearly impossible to identify in advance. TQQQ gave that return automatically.
- **Entry tactic stated:** "I especially like to buy the bullish leveraged ETF on the **first day** of a new QQQQ short-term up-trend."
- **Position management:** "I wade in slowly and only buy more as the index climbs and confirms the trend. Most confident if it reaches 5 days."
- Leveraged ETF advantage over mutual funds: you can exit intraday and use stop losses. Mutual funds typically prohibit market timing and price at end of day only.
- He uses inverse leveraged ETFs (SQQQ) to protect during sustained down-trends — a "two-sided" system.
- Implicit contrast: unlike individual stocks, a leveraged index ETF carries no adverse earnings news or single-company risk.
