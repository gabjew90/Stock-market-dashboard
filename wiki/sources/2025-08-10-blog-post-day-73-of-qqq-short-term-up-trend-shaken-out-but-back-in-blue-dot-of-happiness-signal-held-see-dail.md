# WW 2025-08-10 — Blue dot re-entry after emotional exit; stop losses as sine qua non

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 73 of $QQQ short term up-trend; Shaken out but back in; Blue dot of Happiness signal held; GMI=5 and Green](../../raw/posts/2025-08-10-blog-post-day-73-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-shaken-out-but-back-in-blue-dot-of-happiness-signal-held-see-dail.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2025/08/blog-post-day-73-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-shaken-out-but-back-in-blue-dot-of-happiness-signal-held-see-dail/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

An August 2025 confession post. On July 31, QQQ reversed from all-time highs on heavy volume (red dot); August 1 saw high-volume follow-through selling. Dr. Wish exited his positions even though his stop losses had not been triggered — "a huge mistake." When the market bounced and generated a Blue Dot signal, he slowly bought back in. The post is a candid lesson in emotional trading vs. rules-based trading. It also introduces the concept that breakouts failing (GMI component: 10-Day Successful New Highs <50%) is a warning sign even within an up-trend. Stop losses are described as "sine qua non of trading" — they cannot be skipped. He distinguishes his trading accounts (tactical) from his university retirement accounts (stay in mutual funds through signal confirmation only).

## Key claims

- Exiting before stops are hit (due to emotion) is a mistake — the subsequent bounce proved this in real time.
- The Blue Dot of Happiness signal is the re-entry mechanism after being shaken out.
- "Stop losses are accurately named. They stop my losses. They are the sine qua non of trading." (2025)
- When >50% of stocks reaching new highs 10 days ago are NOT higher today, breakouts are failing — one foot out the door in trading accounts even in an up-trend.
- Pension/retirement accounts follow different rules: stay in mutual funds until the longer-term signals turn down (30-week average, GMI near 0).
- Post-GLB 5-day EMA tracking: red dot signals (high volume reversal) are a warning even before the 5-day EMA is broken.
