# WW 2019-04-07 — GLB applied to IPOs; IIPR example; QQQ trend duration statistics

**Source:** [Trading IPOs with a GLB and a green dot signal: $IIPR; $QQQ short term up-trend is in 61st day](../../raw/posts/2019-04-07-trading-ipos-with-a-glb-and-a-green-dot-signal-iipr-qqq-short-term-up-trend-is-in-61st-day.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2019/04/trading-ipos-with-a-glb-and-a-green-dot-signal-iipr-qqq-short-term-up-trend-is-in-61st-day/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

An April 2019 post applying the GLB concept to recent IPOs, invoking Jesse Livermore's 100-year-old principle of waiting for an IPO to settle and then trading it at an all-time high. IIPR (cannabis REIT) is the worked example: IPO in December 2016, formed a green line top at $20.54, consolidated for about a year, then had a GLB in December 2017 and subsequently quadrupled. FB is a historical comparison: first GLB at $45, 17 months after IPO. The green dot daily signal is shown as a re-entry/secondary entry on daily charts when the GLB has already occurred and the stock has pulled back. The post also includes the updated QQQ short-term trend duration statistics table (through March 2019): down-trends more likely to be short (40% last fewer than 6 days); longest up-trend 88 days; longest down-trend 69 days (back to 2006).

## Key claims

- IPO GLB logic: wait for the IPO to peak, rest for at least 3 months (drawn on monthly chart), then buy on the close above the green line.
- Jesse Livermore: "wait for the IPO to settle and to then trade at an all-time high" — a 100-year-old principle behind the GLB for IPOs.
- Green dot as secondary IPO entry: if you miss the GLB day, look for a green dot bounce on the daily chart while the stock is above its green line and 30-day average.
- QQQ trend duration stats (updated through 2019): about 40% of new down-trends end in fewer than 6 days; 60% of both up- and down-trends last 6–47 days.
- The longest recorded up-trend going back to 1990 was 152 days; going back further to 1930, two down-trends lasted 76 and 88 days.
