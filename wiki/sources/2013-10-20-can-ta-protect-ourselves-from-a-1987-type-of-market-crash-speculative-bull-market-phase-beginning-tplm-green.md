# WW 2013-10-20 — TA vs 1987 crash; 30-day MA as short-term trend signal; speculative bull phase

**Source:** [Can TA protect ourselves from a 1987 type of market crash? Speculative bull market phase beginning? TPLM: green line break-out](../../raw/posts/2013-10-20-can-ta-protect-ourselves-from-a-1987-type-of-market-crash-speculative-bull-market-phase-beginning-tplm-green.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2013/10/can-ta-protect-ourselves-from-a-1987-type-of-market-crash-speculative-bull-market-phase-beginning-tplm-green-line-break-out/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish applies his current indicators retroactively to the 1987 crash to show that technical analysis would have signalled exit well before the crash. He explicitly names the **30-day moving average of closing prices** as the indicator that defines a short-term down-trend: once price breaks below a declining 30-day MA, the short-term trend is down. The analysis also notes that the 30-week average failing and lower lows would have provided a secondary, longer-term signal. The current market (October 2013) has all GMI and GMI2 indicators positive. He raises the thesis of a speculative bull-market phase in progress (blue chips lagging, growth stocks leading).

## Key claims

- **The 30-day MA is the short-term trend signal.** "The 30 day moving average of closing prices (red line) had already curved down… Using my current techniques, by this time, I would have already declared the Dow to be in a short term down-trend." ([WW 2013-10-20](../../raw/posts/2013-10-20-can-ta-protect-ourselves-from-a-1987-type-of-market-crash-speculative-bull-market-phase-beginning-tplm-green.md)) The 1987 crash came on what would have been **Day 9** of the short-term down-trend — plenty of time to exit.
- The 30-week average failing was a secondary warning; the weekly chart would have flagged Stage 4 as well.
- October 2013: GMI=6 of 6, GMI2=8 of 8; QQQ short-term up-trend in day 6; 818 new 52-week highs (last comparable level was November 2010, start of multi-month rally).
- Speculative bull-market phase: Dow blue chips (KO, T, XOM, WMT, MCD) lagging; NASDAQ 100 stocks (87%) above 30wk avg vs Dow 30 stocks (67%). Historically, late-stage bull markets see a rotation from blue chips to riskier growth stocks.
- TPLM had a GLB to an ATH on a monthly chart. ([WW 2013-10-20](../../raw/posts/2013-10-20-can-ta-protect-ourselves-from-a-1987-type-of-market-crash-speculative-bull-market-phase-beginning-tplm-green.md))
