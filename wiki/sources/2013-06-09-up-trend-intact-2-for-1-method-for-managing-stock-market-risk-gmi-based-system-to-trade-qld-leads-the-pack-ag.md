# WW 2013-06-09 — 2-for-1 risk management method; TQQQ outperformance data

**Source:** [Up-trend intact; 2-for-1 method for managing stock market risk; GMI based system to trade QLD leads the pack again; the TSLA break-out](../../raw/posts/2013-06-09-up-trend-intact-2-for-1-method-for-managing-stock-market-risk-gmi-based-system-to-trade-qld-leads-the-pack-ag.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 9 June 2013. Dr. Wish describes the **2-for-1** position management method (from Powell's Books, traced to a Connors trading guide) and applies it to student traders. He also provides concrete data comparing TQQQ to individual stocks during the February–June 2013 GMI buy signal period, reinforcing the leveraged ETF default.

## Key claims

- **The 2-for-1 method:**
  1. Buy a position.
  2. When the position gains enough to double your initial risk (i.e., gains twice the stop distance), **sell half**.
  3. Move the stop on the remaining half up to the **original purchase price** (breakeven).
  4. Result: you have locked in some gain and the remaining half is protected from a loss — you can only gain or break even on the rest.
  - Dr. Wish explicitly says this helps students "minimize losses and retain gains" — the most common failure mode is watching a profit turn into a loss.
- **TQQQ outperformance data (GMI Buy signal Feb 28 to June 7, 2013):** TQQQ advanced +28.6%, QQQ advanced +9.4%. Only 7 of the Nasdaq 100 stocks advanced more than 29% — and most of those moved late (requiring patience). Dr. Wish asks: "Do we want to spend (waste?) our time searching for the 7% of stocks that will out-perform just holding the TQQQ?"
- The GMI-based QLD system was again leading all market-timing systems on the darkliquidity.com rankings at that date (also led in 2012).
- The TSLA post-breakout new-high count: "TSLA has hit a new high on 14 of 49 trading days" since its April 1 breakout — illustrating how true rocket stocks keep appearing on the new-high list.
