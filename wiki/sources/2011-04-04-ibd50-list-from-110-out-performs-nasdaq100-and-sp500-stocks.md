# WW 2011-04-04 — IBD50 vs Nasdaq100/S&P500 systematic comparison; 10-week bounce scan

**Source:** [IBD50 list from 1/10 out-performs Nasdaq100 and S&P500 stocks; Some IBD50 stocks at support](../../raw/posts/2011-04-04-ibd50-list-from-110-out-performs-nasdaq100-and-sp500-stocks.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2011/04/ibd50-list-from-110-out-performs-nasdaq100-and-sp500-stocks/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A quantitative comparison of the IBD50 list (published January 10, 2011) vs the Nasdaq100 and S&P500, measured through late March 2011. Dr. Wish concludes that while a lower percentage of IBD50 stocks rose than Nasdaq100/S&P500 stocks (60% vs 67%/77%), the IBD50 was substantially better at producing 20%+ gainers (18% vs 11%/10%) — even in a period when tech stocks underperformed the broader market. He also demonstrates the 10-week-average bounce scan: finding IBD50 stocks that traded to their 10-week average and bounced, and setting sell stops just below the weekly low.

## Key claims

- IBD50 vs Nasdaq100/S&P500 (Jan 10 – late March 2011, Nasdaq slightly down -0.25%): 60% / 67% / 77% of stocks rose; 18% / 11% / 10% rose 20%+; largest single gainer was SINA +45% (IBD50). ([WW 2011-04-04](../../raw/posts/2011-04-04-ibd50-list-from-110-out-performs-nasdaq100-and-sp500-stocks.md))
- Interpretation: IBD50 is not the best place to find the highest hit-rate, but it is the best place to find the **big winners** — concentrating in 50 stocks instead of 500.
- Caveat: this was a period when tech stocks underperformed the S&P500 (QQQ +2.5%, SPY +4.9%), which would suppress IBD50 performance. Results could be better when tech leads.
- 10-week bounce scan: Dr. Wish scanned all IBD50 stocks he held in his TC2000 watchlist for those that bounced up from their 10-week average the prior week. Table of candidates presented with prior-week low as the sell-stop price. This is the buying method for stocks already in an up-trend.
- GMI at 6, GMI2 at 5 (4wk > 10wk > 30wk alignment not fully present); T2108 at 73% (overbought caution zone); QQQ day U-3 of new short-term up-trend.
