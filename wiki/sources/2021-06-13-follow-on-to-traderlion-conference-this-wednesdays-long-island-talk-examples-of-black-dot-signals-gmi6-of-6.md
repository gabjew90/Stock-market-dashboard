# WW 2021-06-13 — TraderLion conference follow-on; black dot signals; GMI=6

**Source:** [Follow on to TraderLion conference, this Wednesday's Long Island talk; Examples of black dot signals; GMI=6 (of 6)](../../raw/posts/2021-06-13-follow-on-to-traderlion-conference-this-wednesdays-long-island-talk-examples-of-black-dot-signals-gmi6-of-6.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2021/06/follow-on-to-traderlion-conference-this-wednesdays-long-island-talk-examples-of-black-dot-signals-gmi6-of-6/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Post-conference post after Dr. Wish's TraderLion 2021 presentation. Introduces the "black dot" signal — a daily stochastic-based oversold bounce indicator that he uses on individual stocks in up-trends. Provides the TC2000 formula. Shows examples on PGNY and ALLY. Also recaps his use of the QQQ short-term trend count for TQQQ/SQQQ timing, and restates the GMI decision rule with T2108 framing.

## Key claims

- Black dot formula for TC2000: a black dot appears on the daily chart when: (1) the daily 10.1 stochastic was ≤25 in any of the past 3 days; (2) current price > prior day's price; (3) stock is currently above either its 30-day average or its 21-day exponential average.
- Black dots only work on stocks in an up-trend. He looks for stocks that recently hit an ATH, became oversold, and started turning up — placing the stop below the bounce for a minimal loss if it fails.
- "I love a black dot that occurs after a GLB" — his preferred setup is GLB followed by a black dot (oversold bounce), then a green dot (further strength confirmation).
- He uses TQQQ (3× long) and SQQQ (3× short) guided by the QQQ short-term trend count. GMI triggers the broader stance; the short-term count guides the levered ETF.
- GMI decision rule restated: Green if GMI ≥ 4 for two consecutive days; Red if < 3 for two consecutive days. GMI=0 is very bearish.
- T2108 < 10% = look for a major market bottom and slowly buy an index ETF.
- He tweets intraday signals @WishingWealth.
