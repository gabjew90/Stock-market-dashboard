# WW 2009-03-08 — How I use put options as investment insurance

**Source:** [How I use put options as investment insurance](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2009/03/how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written during the March 2009 bear market bottom (T2108 at 7%, 1,221 new lows), Dr. Wish explains how he uses put options to protect long positions instead of, or in addition to, stop-loss orders. He walks through the mechanics of a protective put using GLD as the example: cost, strike selection, and the core advantage — unlike a stop-loss order, a put cannot be triggered by a gap-down and you cannot get whipsawed out of a position by a temporary dip below the stop price.

## Key claims

- A put option gives the holder the right to sell 100 shares at a fixed strike price before expiration, regardless of how far the stock falls. Even if the stock goes to $1, you can still put it at the strike. ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
- The key advantage over a stop-loss: a put **cannot be whipsawed** — if the stock drops below the strike and then rallies, you still hold your shares. A stop-loss would have sold you out. ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
- The cost: the put premium (plus commissions) adds to the break-even price. In his example, a $1.25 put on GLD at the $89 strike means his total max loss rises by ~$125 per 100 shares vs. using a stop. ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
- He can buy puts in his IRA (requires a separate option account approval from the broker). ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
- Analogy: the put premium is like an insurance premium on a house. You do not complain when the house doesn't burn down. ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
- Context: T2108 was at 7% (deeply oversold); 1,221 new lows; QQQQ on day 14 of a short-term down-trend within a longer-term down-trend. He was becoming reluctant to add more shorts. ([WW 2009-03-08](../../raw/posts/2009-03-08-how-i-use-put-options-as-investment-insurance.md))
