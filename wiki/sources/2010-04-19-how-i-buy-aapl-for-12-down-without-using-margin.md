# WW 2010-04-19 — How I buy AAPL for 12% down without using margin

**Source:** [How I buy AAPL for 12% down without using margin!](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2010/04/how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish explains how to use **deep in-the-money (DITM) call options** as a stock substitute — paying a fraction of the stock's full price to control the same number of shares, without margin and without margin interest. He attributes the strategy to Lee Lowell's book and illustrates it with AAPL May $220 calls at $29.40 when AAPL was trading at $247.40 — a 12% effective down payment on a $247 stock.

## Key claims

- A deep in-the-money call has minimal time premium — almost all of its value is intrinsic. A rise in the stock translates nearly dollar-for-dollar into a rise in the option's value. ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- Example: AAPL May $220 call at $29.40 per share controls 100 shares worth ~$24,700 for $2,940 — approximately a 12% down payment. ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- The stock only needs to rise $2 above current price before the option is profitable (because intrinsic value already covers most of the premium). ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- Maximum loss is the premium paid — less than owning the stock outright if the stock falls far below the strike. ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- Critical discipline: use this strategy to control the **same number of shares** one would have bought outright — not more. Using the leverage to size up is how one makes a huge, dangerous bet. ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- Contrast with out-of-the-money options (he calls those gambling — the stock must move far before they have any intrinsic value). ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
- He would not consider buying AAPL calls before earnings — high-fliers often crack on great earnings. ([WW 2010-04-19](../../raw/posts/2010-04-19-how-i-buy-aapl-for-12-down-without-using-margin.md))
