# WW 2005-08-27 — Weinstein Dow sell signal; bank shorts; SBUX

**Source:** [Stan Weinstein Dow sell signal; GMI: +1; Sick bank stocks; SBUX-in the drink?](../../raw/posts/2005-08-27-stan-weinstein-dow-sell-signal-gmi-1-sick-bank-stocks-sbux-in-the-drink.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/08/stan-weinstein-dow-sell-signal-gmi-1-sick-bank-stocks-sbux-in-the-drink/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish opens with a verbatim block quote from Stan Weinstein's 1988 book (pp 270-271): when the Dow closes below its 30-week moving average and enters Stage 4, one must stop all new buys, sell weak holdings, tighten stops, and seek short-sale candidates. He then applies this to August 2005, when the Dow closed below its 30-week average — the first such close since a prior decline. He reviews the full WPM (weekly percentage map) showing all five major index ETFs still above their 30-week averages except the DIA, and uses this to distinguish index-level bear from sector-level bear. The post also introduces the put/call ratio as a contrarian indicator and presents the bank-sector short thesis (rising short-term rates squeezing bank spread) with a detailed Citigroup chart. SBUX is offered as a second short candidate based solely on price and moving average action.

## Key claims

- Weinstein's rule, cited verbatim: Dow below 30-week MA + Stage 4 = "become very defensive"; suspend buying; sell poor relative-strength stocks; tighten stops; hunt short candidates. ([WW 2005-08-27](../../raw/posts/2005-08-27-stan-weinstein-dow-sell-signal-gmi-1-sick-bank-stocks-sbux-in-the-drink.md))
- GMI fell to +1 in August 2005 because the IBD growth mutual fund index closed below its 50-day average; only the QQQQ weekly component needed to fail for a GMI=0.
- Put/call ratio above 0.90 = relatively bullish (contrarian); a ratio well above 1.00 would be more significant.
- Bank stocks short thesis: inverted yield curve (short rates above long rates) would squeeze bank margins; WFC, C, JPM, WB, BAC, and others cited as below their 30-week averages.
- SBUX short: 10-week average crossed below 30-week average, both declining — a sell signal even without news.
- Dr. Wish prefers deep in-the-money puts with 6-9 months duration for IRA shorting.
