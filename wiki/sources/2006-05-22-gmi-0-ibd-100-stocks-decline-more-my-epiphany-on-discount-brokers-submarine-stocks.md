# WW 2006-05-22 — Submarine stock scan criteria; discount broker epiphany; CTX CCL

**Source:** [GMI: 0; IBD 100 stocks decline more; My epiphany on discount brokers; Submarine stocks](../../raw/posts/2006-05-22-gmi-0-ibd-100-stocks-decline-more-my-epiphany-on-discount-brokers-submarine-stocks.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2006/05/gmi-0-ibd-100-stocks-decline-more-my-epiphany-on-discount-brokers-submarine-stocks/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

The most complete published description of Dr. Wish's submarine stock scan. The post also documents the real-money performance of the IBD-100 in a declining market (worse than Nasdaq100 — confirming that growth stocks lead in up-trends and suffer most in down-trends), and shares an insight about cash-interest yield at discount brokers (Fidelity at 4.62% vs Brown & Co. near zero).

## Key claims

- Submarine scan criteria (TC2005, applied to Dr. Wish's 4,000-stock universe): closed below its 10-week average for **7 consecutive weeks** AND the 10-week average is below the 30-week average.
- After finding scan candidates, he visually filters for: (1) peaked at least 4 months ago; (2) peak was far above the prior yearly low (former bull-market leaders); (3) at least one week of atypically large-volume decline since the peak; (4) bounced down off the declining 10-week average in the past few days.
- Entry timing: buy deep in-the-money puts with 6-9 months expiry and minimal time premium. Close if stock trades above its declining 10-week average.
- IBD-100 in a declining market: only 14% of IBD-100 stocks rose in the week of the study; median decline among the 85% that fell was -7.2%, versus -3.5% for Nasdaq-100. Growth stocks amplify both the up-move and the down-move.
- Cash at discount brokers: if going to cash in bad markets, ensure the broker sweeps into a money market fund. Interest foregone at zero-yield firms can exceed commission savings.
- Working examples: CTX (homebuilder, 8-month top-formation before 10wk/30wk cross; purchased housing puts ahead of the sector collapse); CCL given as student exercise.
