# WW 2013-05-20 — TSLA GLB worked example; the full stock-selection workflow

**Source:** [How to find a TSLA like explosive stock before its huge advance; more green line break-outs](../../raw/posts/2013-05-20-how-to-find-a-tsla-like-explosive-stock-before-its-huge-advance-more-green-line-break-outs.md) · tier: trade_example

## What it covers

Written 20 May 2013, with TSLA at $91.50 after its April 2013 breakout from $39.95. Dr. Wish traces the full detection workflow step by step: evening new-high scan → monthly GLB check → weekly chart (volume spike) → daily chart (gap-up). He explains the re-test entry as the preferred low-risk buy point and addresses why IPO stocks are especially attractive for this pattern.

## Key claims

- **Step 1 — Evening scan:** Run a scan of stocks hitting new 52-week highs nightly. TSLA appeared on April 1, 2013.
- **Step 2 — Monthly GLB check:** Immediately look at the monthly chart. TSLA closed at $43.93 on April 1 — well above its prior peak of $39.95 from March 2012 (more than a year earlier). Draw the green line at that prior peak.
- **Step 3 — Weekly chart:** Check for "the highest weekly volume ever." TSLA's weekly volume spike on the breakout week confirmed institutional interest.
- **Step 4 — Daily chart:** Look for a gap-up above the green line on high daily volume. TSLA gapped to a new all-time high on above-average volume on April 1.
- **IPO advantage:** TSLA was a recent IPO in a new growth industry (battery-powered cars). Most funds would not yet own it — therefore a large future buyer base exists.
- **Re-test entry:** TSLA pulled back to the green line ($39.95) after the initial breakout, giving a lower-risk second entry. "It turns out that TSLA did re-test its breakout, giving one an opportunity to buy it at the successful re-test."
- **Exit rule restated:** "If the stock closes back below the green line we can sell with a small loss."
- General principle: "Why are so many people averse to buying stocks that break from a multi-month base (green line top) to a new all-time high? In the stock market I do not want to buy bargains."
