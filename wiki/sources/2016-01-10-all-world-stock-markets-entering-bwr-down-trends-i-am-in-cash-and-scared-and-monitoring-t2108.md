# WW 2016-01-10 — All world stock markets entering BWR down-trends

**Source:** [All World Stock Markets entering BWR Down-trends! I am in cash and monitoring T2108](../../raw/posts/2016-01-10-all-world-stock-markets-entering-bwr-down-trends-i-am-in-cash-and-scared-and-monitoring-t2108.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2016/01/all-world-stock-markets-entering-bwr-down-trends-i-am-in-cash-and-scared-and-monitoring-t2108/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

In January 2016, Dr. Wish applies his RWB/BWR weekly Guppy chart framework across 37 global market ETFs (US, Thailand, Australia, Russia, South Africa, UK, Germany, Hong Kong, France, Chile, India, Egypt, Sweden, China, and more). 35 of 37 were in BWR down-trends; only Belgium and Ireland remained above the BWR threshold. He uses this global co-movement to question whether China alone can be blamed for the decline and floats the idea that commodity deflation may be the common driver. He also explains the BWR pattern's definition more precisely: the weekly close (gray dotted line) is below all 12 averages in a BWR.

## Key claims

- Dr. Wish applies the RWB/BWR framework to global index ETFs as well as US stocks — a global BWR sweep is a bearish macro reading.
- BWR definition refined: the gray dotted line (weekly close) is now below all 12 moving averages; in RWB the gray line is largely above the red averages. A first sign of a new up-trend: the gray dotted line closes back above all 12 averages (though he prefers to see the full RWB develop before trading big).
- 35 of 37 global market ETFs examined were in fully formed BWR down-trends in January 2016. Only Belgium and Ireland were exceptions.
- He includes a T2108 monthly chart in this post: T2108 reached 1 at the 2008 bottom, 7 in 2011, and around 6 in August 2015. T2108 was 15% at the time of this post.
- He promises himself (again) that when T2108 goes below 10 he will buy a small amount of SPY or QQQ — and acknowledges he rarely follows through because the market always looks too scary at the bottom.
- His GMI remains on a Sell signal with all components negative.
