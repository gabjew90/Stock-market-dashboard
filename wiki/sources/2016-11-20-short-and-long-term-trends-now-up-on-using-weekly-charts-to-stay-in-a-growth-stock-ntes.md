# WW 2016-11-20 — Weekly 4wk/10wk hold discipline; NTES and NVDA examples

**Source:** [Short and long term trends now up; On using weekly charts to stay in a growth stock: $NTES](../../raw/posts/2016-11-20-short-and-long-term-trends-now-up-on-using-weekly-charts-to-stay-in-a-growth-stock-ntes.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 20 November 2016. Dr. Wish addresses the most common failure mode he observes in student traders: getting shaken out of a winner prematurely by reacting to daily chart noise. His solution is to make exit decisions using only the **weekly chart** with three simple moving averages (4wk, 10wk, 30wk). NTES and NVDA are the worked examples.

## Key claims

- **The problem:** "One of the enemies of staying with a good stock is focusing on charts with daily and shorter time periods to signal an exit." Daily charts show volatility that looks meaningful but is noise within a strong weekly advance.
- **William O'Neil's approach cited:** Dr. Wish was told that O'Neil "used only weekly charts because they more clearly revealed to him the meaningful stock trends."
- **Weekly hold rule (three MAs: 4wk, 10wk, 30wk):**
  - A strong advancing growth stock maintains 4wk > 10wk > 30wk for long periods, and stays near an all-time high.
  - A weekly close below the **4wk average** is a *potential* sign of weakening — at this point raise stops or sell a small piece.
  - A weekly close below the **10wk average** is an exit signal: "a call for me to exit my position immediately."
  - "Growth stocks should not close the week below their 10 week average."
  - Note: a stock often trades *intraweek* below its 4wk average and then closes back above — use weekly closing prices only, not intraday.
- **NTES example:** NTES showed 4>10>30 alignment from June through November 2016 — 21 weeks. During that time it closed the week below the 4wk average only 3 times, and each time recovered. The stock rose over 40%. When it finally closed below the 4wk and then the 10wk in early November, that was the exit signal.
- **NVDA example:** The same weekly chart approach "would have kept one in NVDA" through its 2016 rocket advance.
- Dr. Wish describes himself as a "part-time trader" who reviews positions over the weekend — this method is designed for that cadence.
- Historical reference: this strategy was described in detail in a 2012 speech to the Houston Worden TC2000 Users group (webinar archived).
