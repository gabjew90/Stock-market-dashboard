# WW 2014-04-27 — Staged pension exit; 30-week trigger; GMI/QQQ divergence

**Source:** [I do not want to be long in this market](../../raw/posts/2014-04-27-i-do-not-want-to-be-long-in-this-market.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 27 April 2014, during a 24-day QQQ short-term down-trend. Dr. Wish provides the most explicit description of his **staged pension exit protocol** and identifies a rare situation: the GMI is on a Buy signal even while the QQQ short-term down-trend is in place — a divergence he treats as a warning. He also explains the macro context (rising interest rates) and his personal investment mandate (most assets are in tax-deferred accounts).

## Key claims

- **Staged 401(k)/pension exit protocol:** "I will do it in stages, maybe 25% at a time."
  - Trigger 1: QQQ closes back below its 30-week average → begin moving from mutual funds to money market, ~25% at a time.
  - Trigger 2: The 30-week average itself starts to decline → "move towards 100% invested in money market funds."
  - Context at time of writing: QQQ at $86.19, 30-week average at $85.93 (just above it); 4-week average already below the 30-week — a sign of early weakening.
- **GMI/QQQ divergence:** GMI on a Buy signal despite 24-day QQQ short-term down-trend. "This is a rare divergence." The QQQ had nearly started a new up-trend the prior week but failed — "this failure was a significant event to me."
- **Speculative positioning:** bought a put option on QID (2× inverse QQQ ETF) in the most speculative trading account as a small bet on a continued down-trend.
- **Boomer panic thesis:** "With the 2000 and 2008 declines in their rear view mirrors, I suspect that most boomers who rode the market up since 2009 are going to flee at the first signs of trouble." Expects a steep decline when one begins. Does not expect a 2008-type, but sees rising Fed rates as the catalyst.
- "I do not like to marry an interesting story or possible scenario for the market." He acts on technicals, not macro narrative.
