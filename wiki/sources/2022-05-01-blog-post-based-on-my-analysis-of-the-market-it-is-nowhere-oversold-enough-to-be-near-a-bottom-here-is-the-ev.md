# WW 2022-05-01 — T2108 monthly; BWR beginning; GLB applied to indexes; 1965–1982 history

Here is the evidence.](../../raw/posts/2022-05-01-blog-post-based-on-my-analysis-of-the-market-it-is-nowhere-oversold-enough-to-be-near-a-bottom-here-is-the-ev.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2022/05/blog-post-based-on-my-analysis-of-the-market-it-is-nowhere-oversold-enough-to-be-near-a-bottom-here-is-the-ev/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A detailed market assessment written at the start of the 2022 bear market. Dr. Wish argues that T2108 at 27% is nowhere near the below-10% level needed to signal a bottom, and presents side-by-side weekly BWR comparison with 2008 showing that the 2022 decline was just beginning to form a BWR pattern. He also applies the GLB concept to index ETFs (DIA, SPY, QQQ all formed green line tops in 2020-21), and draws on a long personal history going back to 1962-1974 to argue that multi-year flat markets regularly follow all-time-high runs.

## Key claims

- **T2108 below 10%** = Dr. Wish's objective signal for an oversold bottom; he then "holds his nose and buys just a little SPY, not individual stocks, accumulate only on the way up after a bottom appears." At the time of the post, T2108 was at 27% — well above that threshold. ([WW 2022-05-01](../../raw/posts/2022-05-01-blog-post-based-on-my-analysis-of-the-market-it-is-nowhere-oversold-enough-to-be-near-a-bottom-here-is-the-ev.md))
- The buy action after T2108 <10%: accumulate SPY (not individual stocks), starting small and only on the way up — not at the very bottom.
- Weekly BWR comparison: SPY in mid-2022 was "just beginning" a weekly BWR (red-below-blue) pattern, paralleling early 2008 in timing.
- GLB concept applied to index ETFs: DIA and SPY formed green line tops (highest monthly close that has not been surpassed for 3+ months) in November 2020; QQQ's last GLB to ATH was June 2020. He writes: "We will be back to a roaring trader's market again when these indexes have another GLB to all-time highs, but it could be months or even years before that."
- Historical context: between 1965 and 1982 the Dow failed repeatedly to break 1,000 — a 17-year period of flatness after prior all-time-high runs. Dr. Wish suggests the same could repeat.
- He does not buy individual stocks when the GMI is Red and remains in cash.
