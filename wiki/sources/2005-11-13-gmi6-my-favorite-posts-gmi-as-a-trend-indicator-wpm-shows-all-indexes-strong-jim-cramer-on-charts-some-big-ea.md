# WW 2005-11-13 — GMI historical track record chart; Cramer vs charts argument

**Source:** [GMI:+6; My favorite posts; GMI as a trend indicator; WPM shows all indexes strong; Jim Cramer on charts; Some big earners at new highs](../../raw/posts/2005-11-13-gmi6-my-favorite-posts-gmi-as-a-trend-indicator-wpm-shows-all-indexes-strong-jim-cramer-on-charts-some-big-ea.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/11/gmi6-my-favorite-posts-gmi-as-a-trend-indicator-wpm-shows-all-indexes-strong-jim-cramer-on-charts-some-big-ea/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

An early post (November 2005, month 7 of the blog) in which Dr. Wish shows a historical chart of his GMI readings from inception, visually demonstrating that periods of GMI > 5 coincide with profitable long-side periods. He also quotes Jim Cramer's anti-chart position from _Jim Cramer's Real Money_ (2005) and refutes it using Cramer's own words. The post concludes with a screen of current stocks at new highs with strong fundamentals.

## Key claims

- GMI historical chart (first ever presented in the blog): shows all periods when GMI was ≥ 5 or fell below; confirms that "it is a good time to be long" when GMI is consistently ≥ 5. GMI had been at 5 or higher since November 1, 2005; was +6 for all of July 2005. ([WW 2005-11-13](../../raw/posts/2005-11-13-gmi6-my-favorite-posts-gmi-as-a-trend-indicator-wpm-shows-all-indexes-strong-jim-cramer-on-charts-some-big-ea.md))
- Cramer (2005, pp 86-87) quoted: "Looking at the chart… can tell you nothing… A chart is never enough to buy a stock. Never." Dr. Wish's rebuttal: two pages later, Cramer himself writes that "stocks anticipate the fortunes of their companies" — meaning chart action precedes news. The 2000 Nasdaq exit and the Enron put-buying both depended on chart analysis.
- Stocks that hit new highs AND are up ≥60% in the year AND had ≥100% quarterly earnings growth are Dr. Wish's strongest candidates. List in November 2005: TIE, LMIA, NWRE, TRAD, IRIS, HANS, ISRG, HUBG, MRVL, VTAL, HOLX, GHL, JLG, SUPX.
- WPM: all five indexes closed above their 30-day and 30-week averages; 80%+ of Nasdaq 100 stocks above 30-day averages — a "market where all types of stocks are participating."
- New-high/new-low ratio: 234 new highs / 44 new lows; 80% of stocks that hit new highs 10 days ago closed higher today.
