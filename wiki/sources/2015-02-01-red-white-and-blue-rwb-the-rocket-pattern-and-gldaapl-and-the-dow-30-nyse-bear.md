# WW 2015-02-01 — Red, White and Blue (RWB): the rocket pattern

**Source:** [Red, White and Blue (RWB)- the rocket pattern and $GLD,$AAPL and the Dow 30; NYSE bear](../../raw/posts/2015-02-01-red-white-and-blue-rwb-the-rocket-pattern-and-gldaapl-and-the-dow-30-nyse-bear.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2015/02/red-white-and-blue-rwb-the-rocket-pattern-and-gldaapl-and-the-dow-30-nyse-bear/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

The original public explanation of the RWB / BWR charting method, explicitly crediting Daryl Guppy's GMMA (Guppy Multiple Moving Average) as the source and explaining the adaptation. Demonstrates the system on GLD (a long RWB then prolonged BWR), AAPL, and the Dow 30 / SPY. Also highlights the NYSE Composite as an early-warning index for major market weakness.

## Key claims

- RWB derives from **Daryl Guppy's GMMA** (Guppy Multiple Moving Average). The 12 EMAs are plotted on weekly charts: **6 shorter-term (red)** at periods 3,5,8,10,12,15 weeks and **6 longer-term (blue)** at periods 30,35,40,45,50,60 weeks, plus a 1-week EMA dotted line showing the current price.
- A strong uptrend = red lines above and separated from rising blue lines (white space between = RWB). A strong downtrend = the reverse (BWR). The same construction applies to daily, hourly, or monthly charts.
- The system works on ETFs and indexes, not only individual stocks. "The challenge is to only buy RWB patterns and short BWR patterns."
- Convergence of the red lines (where 3-week avg ≈ 5-week avg ≈ 10-week avg) signals the stock has been flat — a sign of extreme caution regardless of absolute price level.
- **NYSE Composite as leading indicator:** the NYSE Composite (all US and foreign NYSE stocks, older/less speculative companies) entering Stage 4 in January 2015 while SPY and DIA only just crossed below their 30-week averages. The quicker weakening of established companies can lead the rest of the market into a major decline.
- Entry patience: "Instead of anticipating a change in trend, I prefer to wait until a RWB or BWR pattern is evident."
