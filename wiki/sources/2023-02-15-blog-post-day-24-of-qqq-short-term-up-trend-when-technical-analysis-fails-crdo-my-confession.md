# WW 2023-02-15 — CRDO GLB failure; stops cannot protect against gap-downs; position sizing lesson

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 24 of $QQQ short term up-trend; When technical analysis fails: $CRDO, my confession](../../raw/posts/2023-02-15-blog-post-day-24-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-when-technical-analysis-fails-crdo-my-confession.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2023/02/blog-post-day-24-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-when-technical-analysis-fails-crdo-my-confession/) · tier: trade_example

## What it covers

A February 2023 confession post about CRDO (Credo Technology). CRDO had a GLB at $18.00 on February 7, 2023. Dr. Wish bought a small position. Seven days after the GLB, after the market close, the company announced a major customer was cutting demand — CRDO opened the next day at $10.64, down 46.8%. This is a worked example of the limits of technical analysis and stop-loss orders: "EVEN IF I HAD A HARD SELL STOP IN, IT WOULD NOT HAVE PROTECTED ME. I WOULD HAVE BEEN SOLD OUT AT WEDNESDAY'S OPEN, AROUND $10.64! STOPS DO NOT PROVIDE 100% PROTECTION."

The post distills several lessons: investigate a company before buying; never concentrate too much in a single stock; consider put options to protect large gains; after-hours news can harm any stock; larger, established companies are less vulnerable to this. Dr. Wish notes he found no technical warning in hindsight — the GLB looked clean at entry.

## Key claims

- Hard stops do not protect against gap-down opens triggered by after-hours news — the stock opens at the stop-execution price, not the stop price.
- Even a technically sound GLB can fail due to non-technical (news/company) events.
- Key lesson: do not concentrate too heavily in a single position. Average up once a stock proves itself, rather than buying a large position at breakout.
- Put options are one way to protect a large gain in a concentrated position.
- The stock's headquarters discrepancy (Cayman Islands vs California on different financial sites) was flagged by Dr. Wish's stock buddy as a red flag, but he did not act on it — highlighting the value of fundamental checks.
