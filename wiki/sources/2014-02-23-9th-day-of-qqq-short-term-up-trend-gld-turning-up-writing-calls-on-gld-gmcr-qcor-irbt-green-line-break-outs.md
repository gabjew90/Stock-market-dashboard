## What it covers

Dr. Wish explains his covered-call income strategy on GLD (a gold ETF in a potential Stage 2 up-trend). He provides a detailed worked example of the mechanics — buying GLD shares and selling weekly calls at-the-money to earn time premium (~0.79% per week, ~3.17% per month). He distinguishes why covered calls are appropriate for GLD but not for rocket growth stocks (capped upside). Three concurrent GLBs (GMCR, QCOR, IRBT) are shown on weekly charts. QQQ is in day 9 of a short-term up-trend with GMI=6.

## Key claims

- **Covered call mechanics:** sell weekly call at strike ≈ current price; earn time premium; risk is if stock falls more than premium collected. On GLD at 127.58, selling the 127.50 call at $1.01/share = ~0.79% weekly yield = ~3.17% monthly if repeated.
- **Why not on rocket stocks:** "I do not sell covered calls on growth stocks because if the stock takes off, my gain is capped by the strike price." GLD's low-volatility Stage 2 up-trend makes it suitable; GLB momentum stocks are not. ([WW 2014-02-23](../../raw/posts/2014-02-23-9th-day-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-gld-turning-up-writing-calls-on-gld-gmcr-qcor-irbt-green-line-break-outs.md))
- GMCR (first profiled in 2009 at ATH) broke out of a new green-line base to an ATH; QCOR did similarly; IRBT broke out on large volume.
- QQQ in a "beautiful RWB rocket pattern" on the GMMA chart.
- GMI=6, day 9 of up-trend.
