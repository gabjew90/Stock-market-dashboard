# WW 2009-02-23 — Bear market comparison 1929/1973/1987 vs 2008; duration/depth projections

**Source:** [Comparison of Current Bear to Bear Markets of 1929, 1973-74, 1987 suggests Dow 3,500 possible](../../raw/posts/2009-02-23-comparison-of-current-bear-to-bear-markets-of-1929-1973-74-1987-suggests-dow-3500-possible.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2009/02/comparison-of-current-bear-to-bear-markets-of-1929-1973-74-1987-suggests-dow-3500-possible/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A quantitative analysis comparing the depth of the 2008 bear market against three historical bears (1929, 1973-74, 1987) at equivalent days-past-Dow-peak. Dr. Wish shows that by Day 260 post the 2007 Dow peak, the 2008 decline tracked roughly 14 percentage points below the 1929 decline, making it meaningfully worse than 1973-74 and 1987 at the same point. He concludes that if the 2008 bear continues to track the 1929 pattern (minus 14pp), the Dow could eventually reach ~3,550. The post also provides a teachable moment about why IBD-style growth stocks fail in declining markets.

## Key claims

- By Day 40 after the Dow peak: 1987 had already bottomed at -41% and rebounded; 1929 was down 40%; 1973 and 2007 were down only 4-8% (the 2007 decline was slow to develop). ([WW 2009-02-23](../../raw/posts/2009-02-23-comparison-of-current-bear-to-bear-markets-of-1929-1973-74-1987-suggests-dow-3500-possible.md))
- By Day 260 post peak: the 2007/2008 bear was down ~40%, tracking **greater than** the 1929 decline at that point (-38%), and more than twice the 1973 bear (-17%). This is the key departure from the "current bear is like 1973" consensus at the time.
- The 2008 decline subsequently ran about 14pp smaller than the 1929 decline's trajectory. If that relationship held, a full 1929-style decline of -89% minus 14pp implies a Dow bottom around 3,550.
- Duration projection: the 1973 bear lasted 482 days; the 1929 bear lasted 714 days. At 342 market days post-peak as of the post date, the 2008 bear could extend another 370+ market days.
- Historical observation: major psychological breaks in both 1929 and 1973 bears occurred around Days 420-440 post-peak — suggesting the 2008 decline could accelerate around mid-2009.
- Lesson: Darvas/O'Neil ATH-buying strategies **do not work in declining markets**. "It is so easy to make money buying stocks in a rising market when the GMI is greater than 3."
- GMI and GMI-R at zero as of the post; T2108 at 13% (historically a bottom signal, but not sufficient).
