# WW 2005-06-05 — GMI back to +5; on moving averages

**Source:** [GMI back to +5; Some potential winners; on moving averages](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2005/06/gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Two things in one post. First, a live GMI reading (GMI=5 of 6; QQQQ at 38.10; "day 20 U-20 of the QQQQ up-trend") that illustrates how the indicator is used in practice. Second — and more substantively — a first-principles explanation of moving averages written for a research-psychologist audience: Dr. Wish treats a moving average as a statistical sample, explains why all days in the window count equally, notes that the only way an MA changes is when the new day added differs from the day dropped, and derives three rules he actually uses.

## Key claims

- Three rules for using a moving average: (1) pick the period that has worked for that stock's prior trends; (2) the stock must be trading above the MA; (3) the MA must be rising. ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
- A moving average rises only when today's close is higher than the close being dropped from the window. ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
- He prefers simple (equal-weighted) moving averages; exponential weighting "contradicts the whole idea that we are just computing the average of a sample of closes." ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
- He prefers trading stocks priced $80 or above: more dollar room to pyramid into a 20% move; less susceptibility to manipulation; high price signals sustained demand. ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
- The GMI declined from +6 to +5 because the Successful 10-Day New High count fell below 100 (it was 86). ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
- The QQQ trend count is tracked: "day 20 (U-20) of the QQQQ up-trend." ([WW 2005-06-05](../../raw/posts/2005-06-05-gmi-back-to-5-some-potential-winners-on-moving-averages.md))
