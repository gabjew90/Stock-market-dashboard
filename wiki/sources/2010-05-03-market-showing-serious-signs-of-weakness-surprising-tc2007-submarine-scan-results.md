# WW 2010-05-03 — TC2007 submarine scan on IBD100/New America stocks; PWRD/NTES sector failure

**Source:** [Market Showing Serious Signs of Weakness; Surprising TC2007 Submarine Scan Results](../../raw/posts/2010-05-03-market-showing-serious-signs-of-weakness-surprising-tc2007-submarine-scan-results.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2010/05/market-showing-serious-signs-of-weakness-surprising-tc2007-submarine-scan-results/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A real-time warning post from May 2010, just before the Flash Crash. Dr. Wish applies his submarine scan specifically to former IBD100 and New America stocks (bull market leaders) and finds surprising names. He introduces the principle that stocks begin to decline before fundamental news arrives — reinforcing the case for chart-based entry/exit over news-driven decisions. He also explains the earnings-season refractory period: after earnings releases, many stocks consolidate or decline until the next earnings season begins.

## Key claims

- Submarine scan applied to former IBD100/New America stocks as a universe: looks specifically for fallen leaders. List of 13 (as of May 3, 2010): MSTR, SWN, CVLT, GILD, ATHN, PWRD, NTES, GS, ABVT, SYKE, GOOG, SQM, MCFE. GS and GOOG being on the list was "especially surprising." ([WW 2010-05-03](../../raw/posts/2010-05-03-market-showing-serious-signs-of-weakness-surprising-tc2007-submarine-scan-results.md))
- Sector-pair validation: when two stocks in the same industry fail simultaneously, it confirms a sector-wide decline rather than an idiosyncratic event. PWRD and NTES (both Chinese online gaming) showed identical bearish chart patterns.
- Pattern on the "naked" weekly chart for PWRD: shorter averages (4-week, 10-week) below longer-term 30-week average, and the 30-week average "beginning to curve down." This precedes confirmed Stage 4.
- Earnings-season refractory period: stocks tend to rise into earnings releases; after reporting, they often regroup and decline until the next quarter's anticipation begins.
- Stocks begin declining before the bad fundamental news arrives. "The bad news usually comes out long after a stock has peaked." Dr. Wish relies on the chart pattern, not the news rationale.
- T2108 was at 64% and in a downtrend; Nasdaq-100 MACD-above-signal percentage at 24% (lowest since February) — both early warning indicators. GMI at 5, GMI-R at 7 (not yet decisively bearish).
