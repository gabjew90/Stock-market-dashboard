# WW 2014-09-28 — Biotech scan; news catalyst; AGIO and VRTX examples

**Source:** [This market is not out of the woods; Finding bio-tech stars like $AGIO and $VRTX](../../raw/posts/2014-09-28-this-market-is-not-out-of-the-woods-finding-bio-tech-stars-like-agio-and-vrtx.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 28 September 2014, with GMI at 1 (Sell signal) and QQQ short-term trend turned down. Dr. Wish describes a specialized biotech scan and explains how a fundamental catalyst (clinical trial presentation announcement) combines with the technical unusual-volume alert to identify potential break-out candidates. The post also introduces the 3:45 PM intraday trading rule.

## Key claims

- **Biotech scan:** Dr. Wish runs a TC2000 nightly scan for biotech stocks that advanced that day on **unusually high volume**. This flags stocks where something may be happening before it becomes widely known.
- **News catalyst integration:** being in the research field, he knows that "one schedules public presentations to highlight good results." Reading clinical trial presentation announcements identifies stocks where results are likely positive before they are released. AGIO had a major presentation coming up at a Leerink conference. The next day it "took off."
- **The combination:** technical unusual volume + fundamental news catalyst = pre-breakout alert. The scan finds candidates; the news confirms the catalyst; together they identify potential winners.
- **VRTX historical comparison:** "Unusual trading volume is a clue to finding bio-tech gems like AGIO and even VRTX before they take off."
- **Market context caution:** GMI=1, Sell signal, short-term down-trend begun. Despite the biotech scan, "this week is critical" and he is "unlikely to buy anything right now." Demonstrates discipline: do not override the market-state gate.
- **3:45 PM rule:** "I restrict most of my daily trading to around 3:45 PM when I can estimate where things will close. If I trade earlier in the day I am often whipsawed."
- **Breadth check:** 46 new highs vs 214 new lows — "the opposite of what I would expect from a healthy rising market." He uses this breadth ratio as a confirming signal of market weakness.
