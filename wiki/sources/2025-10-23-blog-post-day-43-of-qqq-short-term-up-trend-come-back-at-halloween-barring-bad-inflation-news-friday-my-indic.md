# WW 2025-10-23 — 4wk weekly bounce entry; 4wk>10wk>30wk alignment; SPY GLB in June

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 43 of $QQQ short term up-trend; "Come Back at Halloween"; Indicators suggest continuing up-trend; weekly chart explanation](../../raw/posts/2025-10-23-blog-post-day-43-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-come-back-at-halloween-barring-bad-inflation-news-friday-my-indic.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2025/10/blog-post-day-43-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-come-back-at-halloween-barring-bad-inflation-news-friday-my-indic/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

An October 2025 post walking through the weekly SPY chart as the clearest illustration of a healthy up-trend: the 4-week average rises above the rising 10-week average, which rises above the rising 30-week average (Weinstein Stage 2). SPY had a GLB to ATH in June 2025. Since then, SPY had closed the week below its 4-week average only twice and then bounced off the 10-week average. Dr. Wish articulates a specific weekly-chart entry: when a stock (or index) closes the week with a bounce up off the 4-week or 10-week average (price bar is green), that is a buy signal — place a stop below the low of that week. He credits weekly charts over daily charts for filtering out whipsaws. The post also invokes the "sell in May, come back at Halloween" seasonality without trading it mechanically.

## Key claims

- The classic Stage 2 up-trend pattern on a weekly chart: 4wk avg rising above rising 10wk, which rises above rising 30wk (= Weinstein Stage 2).
- A week that ends with a bounce up off the 4-week or 10-week average (green bar) is a buy point; stop below that week's low.
- If a stock closes the week below its 4-week average, place the stop below that week's low.
- Weekly charts reduce whipsaw versus daily charts — looking at weekly closes is a preference reinforced in 2025.
- SPY had a GLB to ATH in June 2025 — the GLB-to-ATH concept applies to index ETFs, not just individual stocks.
