# WW 2010-09-27 — Introducing Red White and Blue (RWB) Stocks

**Source:** [Introducing Red White and Blue (RWB) Stocks–The Pattern of Rockets](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2010/09/introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish coins the Red White and Blue (RWB) pattern — the visual signature of a rocket stock on a weekly Guppy Multiple Moving Average (GMMA) chart. He attributes the color scheme to a student named Marcus and applies it to weekly GMMA charts: the shorter-term moving averages are red, the longer-term are blue, and the white space between rising red and rising blue averages marks a genuine rocket. Three examples are given (CMI, LOGM, IGTE). He also mentions that the T2108 was near overbought levels (~80%) but was not as predictive at high readings as at extreme low readings (below 10%).

## Key claims

- RWB = on a weekly GMMA chart, the shorter-term averages (red) are above the longer-term averages (blue) with white space between them, both sets rising. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
- The opposite pattern — shorter-term below longer-term — is BWR ("submarine stock"). ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
- "I never can understand why most people who want to own a rocket, refuse to buy stocks trading at highs. A rocket on the way to the moon has to hit many daily new highs along the way." ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
- T2108 was ~80% at this time (near overbought), but a high T2108 is "not as predictive as an extremely low reading, below 10%." ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
- The GMI was at 6 of 6, the QQQ short-term up-trend was on day 14. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
- 71% of stocks that hit a new high 10 days ago closed higher on Friday — the "Successful New High Index" reading. ([WW 2010-09-27](../../raw/posts/2010-09-27-introducing-red-white-and-blue-rwb-stocks-the-pattern-of-rockets.md))
