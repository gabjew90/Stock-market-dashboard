# WW 2017-03-26 — Refined strategy for timing exits and entries (TLC/RLC/BLC)

**Source:** [Market rally over? My refined strategy for timing exits and entries; Recent GLB: $LITE](../../raw/posts/2017-03-26-market-rally-over-my-refined-strategy-for-timing-exits-and-entries-recent-glb-lite.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2017/03/market-rally-over-my-refined-strategy-for-timing-exits-and-entries-recent-glb-lite/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Introduces three quantitative measures for reading the daily RWB chart: Total Line Count (TLC), Red Line Count (RLC), and Blue Line Count (BLC). These extend the binary RWB/BWR classification to a 0–12 numeric scale that captures partial trend degradation before a full breakdown. Also introduces a specific TC2000 scan combining Bollinger Band lower-band bounces (or low stochastics) with a full-RWB lineup as a buy trigger.

## Key claims

- **TLC (Total Line Count):** count of the 12 EMAs that the close is above, with each successive average higher than the next longer one. A perfect TLC=12 means the stock is above all 12 averages, which are themselves in order (RWB pattern intact at daily resolution). Displayed as TLC/RLC/BLC on the chart header, e.g., "12/6/6."
- **RLC (Red Line Count):** 0–6, the number of the 6 shorter-term (red) EMAs the close is above. RLC=6 = fully above all red lines. When RLC drops to 0 the first partial exit or position reduction is warranted.
- **BLC (Blue Line Count):** 0–6, the number of the 6 longer-term (blue) EMAs the close is above. If RLC=0 but BLC=6, the stock is still in an overall uptrend — caution but not immediate exit. If any red line crosses below a blue line, the white space disappears and he "must sell the stock."
- **Buy scan logic:** look for stocks in an RWB up-trend (TLC=12) whose RLC has just recovered back to 6 after a period of weakness, AND which had a lower Bollinger Band bounce OR low stochastics in the last 5 days. This identifies pullback-and-recovery entries.
- **Mental stops on daily charts:** prefer to avoid whipsaws by assessing positions near the close each day and using mental (not hard) stops. The purple-dot (daily low) overlay helps identify where support has held.
- ETF-level readings as market health: SPY 6/0/3 and DIA 6/0/4 in March 2017 (below all red lines, weakening); QQQ 9/1/6 (still above blue lines, RWB weakened but intact). QQQ health determines GMI color.
