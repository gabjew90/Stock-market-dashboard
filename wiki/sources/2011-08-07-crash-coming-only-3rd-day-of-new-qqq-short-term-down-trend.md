# WW 2011-08-07 — GMI warning before 2011 flash crash; short-term down-trend significance

**Source:** [Crash coming? Only 3rd day of new QQQ short term down-trend](../../raw/posts/2011-08-07-crash-coming-only-3rd-day-of-new-qqq-short-term-down-trend.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2011/08/crash-coming-only-3rd-day-of-new-qqq-short-term-down-trend/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish posts on the weekend before the August 2011 market decline, assessing the situation through his indicators. The GMI had been below 4 since July 27, prompting defensive posture. He explains why down-trends can extend further than expected and references a long-history bear-market indicator that has been below 20 at almost all major market bottoms since 1933 (not further identified). He references the GMMA chart as showing the decline is more like 2010 consolidation than 2008, providing perspective. T2108 at 11%.

## Key claims

- GMI had been below 4 since July 27, and below 3 for almost every day in that period — his trading accounts were "almost 100% in cash" already. ([WW 2011-08-07](../../raw/posts/2011-08-07-crash-coming-only-3rd-day-of-new-qqq-short-term-down-trend.md))
- "Most disturbing to me is that my short term trend count for the QQQ is only day 3 of the new down-trend. Down-trends often last for many days."
- A long-history indicator he does not name has been below 20 at almost all major bear market bottoms back to 1933; it was at 83 during this post. (Likely T2108 or a similar breadth indicator.)
- The GMMA weekly chart shows shorter-term averages still above longer-term averages — more like 2010 consolidation than 2008 bear. He urges balanced perspective against hysteria.
- T2108 at 11% — in oversold territory but not at extreme lows of 2001/2002 (7%) or 2008 (<1%). More room to fall if multi-month decline develops.
- Pension still invested in mutual funds (couldn't exit without triggering timing penalties — an important caveat on GMI discipline for pension accounts).
