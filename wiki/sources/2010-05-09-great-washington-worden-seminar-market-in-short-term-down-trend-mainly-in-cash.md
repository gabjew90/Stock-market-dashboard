# WW 2010-05-09 — Washington Worden seminar; submarine scan; Stage 2 pension rule

**Source:** [Great Washington Worden Seminar; Market in short term down-trend; Mainly in cash](../../raw/posts/2010-05-09-great-washington-worden-seminar-market-in-short-term-down-trend-mainly-in-cash.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 9 May 2010, after the May 5–6 market drop (prelude to the May 6 Flash Crash). Dr. Wish recaps presenting his TC2007 workflow to ~120 attendees at a Worden software workshop. He then explains his current defensive posture: GMI and GMI-R both at 1; QQQ in a short-term down-trend (Day 3); T2108 dropped from 64% to 15% — near historical bounce territory. Explains the Stage 2 pension rule with explicit precision.

## Key claims

- **Stage 2 pension rule stated precisely:** he only transfers pension funds to money market when the QQQ weekly trend enters Stage 4 — defined as when the 30-week average has curved down. As long as the 30-week average is still rising (Stage 2), the pension stays fully invested in mutual funds, even during a short-term down-trend. He would "dollar cost average in with new contributions."
- **Short-term down-trend confirmation threshold:** he will be "more certain" a new down-trend is in place if it lasts 5 days. "Once we pass that point, trends tend to last for a while."
- T2108 at 15%: notes prior bottoms have occurred in this range. Historical reference: T2108 touched 6% at the March 2009 bottom re-test, and 1% at the October 2008 panic low. At 15% he is "not beginning to short stocks."
- **IBD100 vs Nasdaq 100 in down-trends:** in the brief period from 4/9 to 5/7/2010, more than half of IBD100 stocks declined 9.5% or more, while the QQQ only declined 7.4%. Growth stocks tend to fall faster in down-trends. But in the up-phase (4/9–4/26) 50% of IBD100 advanced 4.9%+, vs QQQ +2.8%. So IBD100 stocks amplify both directions.
- **Submarine scan validation:** ran the scan on April 29; all 9 resulting stocks declined; 33% declined 17%+ (vs only 5% of Nasdaq 100 at that level). "At least that time, the submarine scan did select stocks with a greater likelihood of sinking."
- Self-described trading style: "I trade like a chicken, run from a down-trend, and conserve my capital to trade only when the odds are in my favor."
