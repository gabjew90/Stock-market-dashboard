# WW 2012-04-30 — How I find the next AAPL growth stock; new GMI buy signal

**Source:** [How I find the next AAPL growth stock; New GMI buy signal; IBD50 out-performs again](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2012/04/how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Dr. Wish makes the case for buying stocks at all-time highs using AAPL's 2011–2012 run as the example. He traces AAPL's daily new-high pace (1 in Jan, 13 in Feb, 15 in March, 4 in April), then describes his approach: focus on monthly charts showing multi-month consolidations followed by breakouts to new highs. He also explains his GMI buy signal criterion (GMI > 3 on two consecutive days) and presents data showing the IBD 50 significantly outperformed the NASDAQ 100 and S&P 500 in the 2011–2012 rally.

## Key claims

- Anyone who refuses to buy a stock at an all-time high will never ride a great winner. "A stock that climbs to the sky has to hit new daily highs many times along the journey." ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- To find the next AAPL: start from the list of 52-week highs, weed out those not near their all-time high, then research the remaining ones' fundamentals and technicals. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- His focus when the GMI gives a buy signal: stocks at or near their all-time high, up strongly for months, breaking out of a **multi-month consolidation**. Inspected on the monthly chart. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- Example stock: PSMT, bought at $80 on a monthly breakout from a 6–7 month consolidation after a 7-month run that doubled its price. He bought a small pilot buy at the breakout with a stop below that level. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- He "never averages down" and loves to "pay more for a stock I have already bought" (adds as it rises). ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- GMI buy signal criterion (2012): GMI > 3 on **two consecutive days**. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- IBD 50 outperformance data from 12/22/2011 to 4/2012: IBD 50 median gain +20% vs NASDAQ 100 +15% vs S&P 500 +10%. 34% of IBD 50 gained ≥30%, vs 16% of NASDAQ 100 and 9% of S&P 500. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
- He also sold cash-secured puts on SPY as a separate tactic to earn weekly income — only willing to be put SPY, not individual volatile stocks. ([WW 2012-04-30](../../raw/posts/2012-04-30-how-to-find-the-next-aapl-growth-stock-new-gmi-buy-signal-ibd50-out-performs-again.md))
