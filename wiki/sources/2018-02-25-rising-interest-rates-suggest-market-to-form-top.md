# WW 2018-02-25 — Monthly RWB on bond ETFs; Martin Zweig Fed tightening principle

**Source:** [Rising interest rates suggest market to form top](../../raw/posts/2018-02-25-rising-interest-rates-suggest-market-to-form-top.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 25 February 2018, after the January/February 2018 correction. Dr. Wish applies the RWB/BWR framework to **monthly** bond ETF charts as a long-term interest-rate regime indicator, and discusses Martin Zweig's principle that Fed rate hikes eventually cause equity market tops. He describes monitoring bond ETFs as a macro confirmation tool alongside the GMI.

## Key claims

- **Martin Zweig principle:** after the Fed raises rates several times, the market eventually declines. People believe the market can survive, then eventually surrender to rising rates. This is a multi-cycle pattern Dr. Wish has observed since the 1960s.
- **Monthly RWB on bond ETFs:** he applies the Guppy/GMMA chart not just to stocks but to long-term monthly bond ETF charts. In a low-rate environment, bond ETFs show an RWB pattern. When that pattern transitions to BWR, it signals a sustained rate rise.
- **SHY (short-term bond ETF) specific observation:**
  - SHY showed an RWB pattern "through about 2013" (low-rate era).
  - By mid-2016, SHY entered a BWR decline on the monthly chart — "for the first time since at least 2003." Monthly BWR on bonds "suggests this trend could persist for months if not years."
- **Interest rate chart:** TC2000 offers a direct chart of short-term interest rates. As of February 2018, rates were in a steep RWB up-trend — rising sharply from 2015 lows.
- **New-high count warning:** on Friday before the post, only 90 US stocks hit new highs. Prior to January 29, there were more than 200 per day — breadth deteriorating.
- **Current stance:** GMI still Green (SPY and QQQ closed above 10-week averages for 2nd week in a row), but "I am willing to own some stocks but keeping one foot out the door."
- **Caveat:** this is a monitoring framework, not a trading rule. He still defers to the GMI for actual buy/sell decisions.
