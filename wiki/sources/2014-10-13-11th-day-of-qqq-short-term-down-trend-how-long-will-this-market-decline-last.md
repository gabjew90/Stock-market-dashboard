# WW 2014-10-13 — 11th day of QQQ short-term down-trend; how long will this decline last?

**Source:** [11th day of $QQQ short term down-trend; How long will this market decline last?](../../raw/posts/2014-10-13-11th-day-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-how-long-will-this-market-decline-last.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2014/10/11th-day-of-qqq-short-term-down-trend-how-long-will-this-market-decline-last/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A teaching post written during a 2014 October correction (Day 11 of a QQQ short-term down-trend), answering the question of how he reads six signals to assess whether the decline is a temporary pullback or the beginning of a Stage 4. He explains stage analysis, the GMI, T2108, the put/call ratio, IBD Market Pulse, and his "get out if uncomfortable" principle. Provides the clearest non-2014-08-03 source for the stage analysis all-4-stages description.

## Key claims

- Stage analysis a la Weinstein: Stage 2 = above and following a rising 30-week average. "If they remain below these averages and the averages turn down, the market could be at the beginning of a major Stage 4 decline."
- He describes Stage 4 explicitly here: a sustained declining market, confirmed when the index is below its 30-week average and that average has turned down. He transfers his pension money out of mutual funds in stages when a Stage 4 decline threatens.
- T2108 at 14% in October 2014 — not yet at single digits. He notes it reached 13% in 2013's worst point, 7% in the large 2011 decline. When T2108 gets into single digits he "must fight my emotions and fly on instruments" and make a small initial buy of an index ETF.
- Put/call ratio: when it approaches 1.2 (120 puts per 100 calls), a contrarian bounce signal. On the Friday of this post, IBD reported 1.16.
- IBD Market Pulse: he uses it alongside the GMI as corroboration (different methodology, frequently agrees).
- Exit logic: if you get out at price X and the market declines, you profit as long as you re-enter below X. You do not need to identify the exact bottom.
