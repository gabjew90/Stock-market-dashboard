# WW 2023-11-26 — Canonical GLB re-statement with PGR worked example

**Source:** [Very Important Blog Post explaining GLBs: Day 15 of $QQQ short term up-trend; $PGR is an example of a successful GLB](../../raw/posts/2023-11-26-very-important-blog-post-explaining-glbs-day-15-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-123-us-new-highs-and-2-lows-43-at.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2023/11/very-important-blog-post-explaining-glbs-day-15-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-123-us-new-highs-and-2-lows-43-at/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A November 2023 post explicitly flagged "very important" — a canonical re-statement of the GLB definition for a broad audience. The green line is defined as: "the price bar during which an ATH was reached that was not exceeded for at least 3 months (bars)" on a monthly chart. PGR is the worked example: GLB on October 13 on heavy volume; never closed back below the green line; up 9.2% from the green line by late November. IBD/MS Composite rating=96 is checked before buying. The post names additional recent GLB stocks (CMG, MSFT, DUOL, MPTI, QLYS, HLT, CAH, CRH, WING, RACE, V, COR, AIR, ITT, L, CNM). The exit rule is restated plainly: "I sell immediately if it closes back below the green line." The post explicitly criticizes buying at lows as inferior: "Most people think it is better to buy stocks at lows — WRONG!!!"

## Key claims

- Canonical GLB definition (2023): green line drawn on monthly chart at the ATH level that was not exceeded for at least 3 months. TC2000 alert set to notify when the stock trades above it.
- GLB only works in market up-trends — buying in down-trends leads to failed breakouts.
- Exit rule: sell immediately if the stock closes back below the green line. Many times it trades below intraday but recovers above by close — do not exit on intraday dips.
- After missing a GLB day, still acceptable to buy at a later entry if the stock exhibits another entry setup above the green line.
- "I only buy stocks trading above their last green line tops" — stocks below their most recent ATH have overhead supply.
- IBD/MS Composite rating is checked as a fundamental filter before buying.
