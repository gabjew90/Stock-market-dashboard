# WW 2023-04-24 — WING missed GLB confessions; written GLB rules; TC2000 alert workflow

**Source:** [Blog Post: Day 26 of $QQQ short term up-trend; $WING flies to ATH, how I missed the GLB — true confessions, and see my green line rules](../../raw/posts/2023-04-24-blog-post-day-26-of-qqq-short-term-up-trend-wing-flies-to-ath-how-i-missed-the-glb-true-confessions-and-see-m.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 24 April 2023, with QQQ on Day 26 of a short-term up-trend. Dr. Wish confesses missing the WING GLB despite having set TC2000 alerts — and being alert-alerted three times on April 18. He uses the failure as a teaching moment to publish his written GLB rules for students. He also presents APLS as a contrasting successful GLB example.

## Key claims

- **TC2000 alert workflow:** He draws a green line on monthly charts and immediately sets a TC2000 price alert — good for one year — to notify him when the stock trades back above the green line. The alert is sent as a text message.
- **WING: the missed GLB story:** he had received 3 alerts on April 18 (because he had mistakenly set multiple alerts). He "NEVER saw these alerts" and missed the breakout. "I was too busy with other things and too down on how the market was behaving." Self-described as "embarrassing."
- **Multiple failed GLBs before the real one:** WING had several prior failed GLBs. The rule: "sometimes this dance goes along for a while until the stock finally takes off, often without me." WING's actual breakout (March 18, 2023) was on above-average volume; the stock moved from ~$187 and was at ATH by late April.
- **Turtle Traders rule cited:** the Turtle Traders had a rule that "they had to act on every buy signal. The one signal you do not take after several fails is often the one that works." This is the behavioral lesson from the missed WING trade.
- **Written GLB rules for students (published in post):**
  - Draw a green line at a stock's peak once it has not been surpassed for at least 3 months.
  - Set a TC2000 alert (valid 1 year) to notify when the stock trades back above the green line.
  - Buy on the day of the GLB (or watch for a re-test). May buy a little and see if the stock continues.
  - Hold unless the stock **closes** back below the green line. Many times a stock trades below the green line intraday but closes back above — do not exit on intraday dips.
  - If it closes below the green line: failed GLB, exit immediately.
  - If it retakes the green line and closes back above it: buy it back.
- **APLS contrasting example:** a "shining example" of a successful GLB that broke out and ran without looking back — presented as what to expect when a GLB works.
- Principle: "Don't waste your time trading the fallen angels trading well down from their all-time highs. Focus on those climbing to new heights."
