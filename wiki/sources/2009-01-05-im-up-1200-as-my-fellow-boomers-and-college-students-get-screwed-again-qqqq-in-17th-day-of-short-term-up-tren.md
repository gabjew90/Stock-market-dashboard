# WW 2009-01-05 — 1200% IRA return; capital conservation; pension vs trading IRA

**Source:** [I'm up 1200%, as my fellow boomers and college students get screwed again](../../raw/posts/2009-01-05-im-up-1200-as-my-fellow-boomers-and-college-students-get-screwed-again-qqqq-in-17th-day-of-short-term-up-tren.md) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

Written 5 January 2009, this post gives Dr. Wish's clearest early statement of his performance track record and core philosophy. He escaped 2008 with only a −5% loss (vs indices −35–40%) by being mainly out of the market during the decline. His trading IRA is up over 1,200% (13×) since late 1994 — more than the top growth mutual fund in IBD's ranking (up 382% over the same period). The key principle stated: make money during up-trends and **keep it** by being out during down-trends. He also details the pension vs. trading IRA structure and the cash-secured puts tactic he uses during early recovery.

## Key claims

- "The key to my success is that I made money during the strong market rises and kept it, by being mainly out of the market during the major declines." Trading IRA up ~1,200% (13×) since late 1994.
- In both the 2000–2002 and 2008 declines he avoided major losses in the **university pension** by transferring from mutual funds to a money market fund — a separate discipline from the trading IRA.
- "Conservation of capital during the inevitable market down-trends" is described as the lesson learned over 40+ years.
- Even at GMI=2 (below the long threshold), he was "nibbling at IBD100 stocks at new highs" in the trading IRA using **cash-secured puts** and small long positions during the short-term up-trend.
- IBD100 stocks were ~3× over-represented among stocks hitting new 52-week highs at the time — 50% of the 20 new-high stocks in his 4,000-stock universe were from IBD100 (which is only 17% of the universe).
- He owned a small QLD (2× QQQ ETF) position and planned to hold until the short-term up-trend ended.
