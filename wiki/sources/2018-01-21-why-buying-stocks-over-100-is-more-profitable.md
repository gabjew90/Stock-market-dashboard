# WW 2018-01-21 — Why buying stocks over $100 is more profitable

**Source:** [Why buying stocks over $100 is more profitable](../../raw/posts/2018-01-21-why-buying-stocks-over-100-is-more-profitable.md) · [original](https://wishingwealthblog.com/2018/01/why-buying-stocks-over-100-is-more-profitable/) · tier: teaching

## What it covers

A dedicated post on the $100+ price preference, with both quantitative evidence and qualitative reasoning. Dr. Wish traces the idea to Richard Wyckoff and Jesse Livermore. He provides a concrete data point: 85% of the top 20 largest point-gainers over the prior 30 trading days were priced at $100 or more 30 days before the gain. He also explains the psychological dimension of trading higher-priced stocks: easier to pyramid, easier to take small losses in percentage terms, and the dollar impact of a move feels more controllable.

## Key claims

- Richard Wyckoff observation (quoted): a stock at $150 averages 2.5 points fluctuation per day vs. a $50 stock averaging only 1 point — 2.5× more action in the higher-priced stock.
- Empirical evidence from TC2000: among 4,865 US stocks, the top 20 point-gainers over the prior 30 trading days — those that gained >$54 — were **85% priced over $100** thirty days before the gain. The 3 exceptions were SAGE ($89.79), MDGL ($46.30), and WINS ($82.94). The same relationship held for the next 20 large-gainers: **95% were over $100**.
- William O'Neil: a 20% move in a $20 stock = $4 gain; a 20% move in a $100 stock = $20 gain. Same percentage, very different dollar outcome — which matters for pyramiding.
- Five reasons he likes expensive stocks: (1) institutions bid them up; (2) harder to manipulate/whipsaw; (3) time to accumulate in stages as it rises; (4) smaller universe to monitor; (5) more money when right.
- "By the way, this strategy is antithetical to most inexperienced traders' dreams of buying cheap stocks that will rocket to the moon."
- The $100+ preference is antithetical to the mental bias that cheap stocks have more upside room (a common error in thinking about stocks in percentage vs. dollar terms).
- He applies this strategy especially with call options: a 100-share call option on a $300 stock rising many points generates large dollar gains for a well-timed entry.
